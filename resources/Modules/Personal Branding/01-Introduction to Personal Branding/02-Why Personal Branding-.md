# Why Personal Branding?

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/OPGm-4v7wvs)

Before you start working on your personal brand, it’s crucial to reflect on the following questions:

- **What is your status quo? **Assess your current social media influence on your active accounts. Record the number of followers you have, the average impressions per post, and daily or weekly profile visitors. Also, keep track of other relevant metrics you wish to impact through your personal brand building, like monthly revenue
- **What is your desired outcome?**
Define what you aim to accomplish with your personal brand. This could range from recruiting new team members, boosting product sales, to securing investment. Break down these goals into both qualitative and quantitative targets.
- **Who are you trying to reach?**
Once you know what your goal is, you can answer who it is that you are trying to reach.  For example, if seeking investment, what kind of investors are you targeting? Or, if hiring, what skills and knowledge should potential employees possess?
- **Where does my ideal audience spend their time?**
To determine the most effective platform and content format for your target audience, it's crucial to know where they spend their time. While extensive research isn't necessary, use your best judgment in this assessment. For instance, if your audience includes younger female demographics, TikTok might be your go-to, whereas LinkedIn would be more appropriate for engaging with investors.
- **Who are you?**
An existential question, but an important one to answer. By doing so, you discover what content you can authentically produce. Consider your own story and what distinguishes you among the many other entrepreneurs working to build their personal brands.
