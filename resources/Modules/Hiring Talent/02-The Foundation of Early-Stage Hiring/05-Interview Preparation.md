# Interview Preparation

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/sghMNihP6mM)

Once you and your team have aligned on what role needs to be filled and what it will entail, the next step is preparing the hiring process itself. At this stage, structure matters: you’re moving from “what we need” to “how we’ll find and evaluate the right person.”

A strong preparation process usually involves five key components:

## 1. Role Documentation

Translate the decision into two clear documents:

- **Internal role brief** – captures contribution, accountability, and alignment with team goals.
- **External job vacancy** – communicates requirements and sells your startup to potential candidates.

## 2. Interview Structure and Materials

Plan the candidate journey. Decide which interviews or assessments to use — screening calls, case studies, technical reviews, presentations — and map them into rounds. Prepare materials and communication templates in advance, so the process runs smoothly once candidates enter the funnel.

## 3. Distribution Funnel

Design your sourcing strategy. Go beyond your immediate network by leveraging employees, advisors, investors, and communities (Slack, WhatsApp, LinkedIn). Treat it like a campaign: track outreach, follow up, and make sure amplifiers actually share the role.

## 4. Candidate Funnel

As applications come in, keep them organised. Track who is in which stage — screening, first interview, second round — using a central document or application tracking system (ATS). This ensures transparency and avoids candidates slipping through the cracks.

## 5. Scorecards and Final Decision

For the final shortlist (ideally 2–5 candidates), use structured scorecards to evaluate strengths and weaknesses against the role’s must-haves. This helps reduce bias, provides a fair basis for discussion, and ensures the team makes a high-quality, confident decision.
