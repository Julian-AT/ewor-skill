# The Hiring Blueprint & Manual

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/yxLG54jZRXI)

By now, we’ve covered when to hire, who to bring in early, how to define roles, and how to prepare both your internal team and external postings. You’ve also seen how to structure interviews and start managing your hiring funnel. Before you move further into actually running these processes, it’s worth pausing to look at a resource that ties it all together: the Hiring Manual.

This manual is designed to help you assess exceptional talent. Use it to identify high-magnitude individuals whose mindset, track record, and trajectory match the ambition and complexity of building a world-changing venture. It doesn’t just cover the mechanics — interviews, case studies, references — but also shows how to weave them into a process that consistently produces top-tier hires.

## A Walkthrough of the Hiring Manual

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/V8sdH81kE_Q)

This video walks you through EWOR’s Hiring Manual step by step. While the full process may look heavy at first glance, you don’t need to apply everything to every hire. Even so, by your 3rd–5th hire, it’s already worth using parts of this structure to stay objective and organised.

**The Takeaway Key Steps **

1. **Define the Role Clearly**
Start by identifying whether the hire is DNA-first or Resume-first. Write down the bottleneck or opportunity you’re addressing, the two to three must-haves, and the expected outcome.
2. **Draft the Vacancy**
Use the manual template to create both an internal role brief and an external posting. This ensures alignment inside the team while also presenting the role compellingly to candidates.
3. **Prepare Assessments and Materials**
Decide on the interviews, case studies, technical tasks, and reference calls you’ll use. Prepare supporting materials and email templates in advance so the process runs smoothly.
4. **Fill the Funnel**
Activate your network of employees, advisors, investors, and communities. Track outreach systematically so you know who has posted, shared, or referred candidates.
5. **Manage the Candidate Funnel**
Keep a central record of candidates across rounds (screening, first interview, second interview, etc.), with notes on why they moved forward or dropped out.
6. **Use Scorecards**
For final candidates, create scorecards around must-haves, values, and key strengths. These don’t add up to a numeric total but instead create a structured basis for team discussion and a more objective final decision.
