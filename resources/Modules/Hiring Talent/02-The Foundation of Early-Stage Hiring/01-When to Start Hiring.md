# When to Start Hiring 

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/ya4gea_Jiag)

In the pre-PMF stage, uncertainty is still high. You are testing, pivoting, and searching for the right model. Because of this, your team should remain as lean as possible. 

- If you are two co-founders, avoid hiring more than three additional people.
- With three or four co-founders, one or two hires are enough. 

Once you exceed five people, communication channels multiply and complexity rises quickly. 

![Where n = the number of people ](/api/media/file/number%20of%20channels%20equation.png)

However, at this point, founders should still be able to oversee everything, moving between high-level strategy and hands-on execution in a so-called **“falcon mode.”** This means being able to zoom out to set direction while still diving into the details to close a customer or improve the product. Hiring too many people too early makes it harder to maintain this agility and creates three big risks.

1. **Cost culture**: salaries drain limited runway. Frugality often makes the difference between survival and failure in the early days.
2. **Inefficiency**: onboarding new hires takes time, and on average, employees need six months before becoming net-positive for the company.
3. **Premature management**: stepping into a manager role too early means you stop building deep knowledge of your product, your code, and your customers. Without that foundation, you will struggle to evaluate, delegate, and push back effectively later.
