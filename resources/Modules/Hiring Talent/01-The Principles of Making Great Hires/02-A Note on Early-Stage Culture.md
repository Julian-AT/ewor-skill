# A Note on Early-Stage Culture

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/zV2eaAwwIGc)

When you’re just starting out, culture can feel like a fuzzy, abstract concept. In reality, early culture is nothing more than your values as founders translated into how you build and run the company.

# Culture Starts with You

At the very beginning, your culture is simply a mirror of who you are and how you operate:

- If you have a relentless work ethic, you’ll naturally seek people who match that intensity.
- If you hold yourself to high standards of exceptionalism, you’ll want teammates who do the same.
- If you hate long meetings, your company will naturally evolve into a no-meeting culture.

## An Introspective Exercise

Your job as founders is to take an honest look at:

1. **How you work.** What do you reward, tolerate, or reject in yourselves?
2. **What you value.** What principles guide your decisions?
3. **How you want to build.** What habits or rules define your ideal working style?

## Keep It Simple

At this stage, you don’t need lengthy culture decks, corporate slogans, or a library of books on management. Your culture is already there — it’s you. The only task is to recognise it, name it, and then **hire people who align with it.**
