# Introduction to Hiring

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/XB-en__ztW8)

Hiring is one of the most important — and hardest — things you’ll do as a founder. Your earliest hires will shape your culture and set your startup’s trajectory, so it’s vital to approach the process with care. This module will guide you through when to hire, how to define roles, build and manage a funnel, run interviews, and eventually create a process that scales.

But before we dive into the details, there are four key principles to keep in mind as you go about this process:

- **Network:** Early hires almost always come from people you already know and trust. These connections give you a foundation of speed and reliability that’s hard to find elsewhere.
- **Generalists: **In the beginning, you benefit most from people who can stretch across different roles, adapt to change, and stay steady when priorities shift.
- **Quality:** The first few people will set the tone for your culture. It’s worth paying more or offering more equity if it means bringing in the strongest possible talent.
- **Simplicity:** At this stage, hiring doesn’t need complex systems. If you’re confident in someone, move quickly and keep the process straightforward.

For now, keep this in mind: your earliest hires will define your culture and shape the trajectory of your startup. Keep it simple, move quickly, and bring in only the very best.
