# Onboarding Employees

Onboarding plays a crucial role in shaping how new hires perceive you, their team, and the organisation as a whole, setting the stage for their future interactions and experiences. When done effectively, onboarding creates a positive and lasting impression while fostering alignment from the very beginning. In this topic, we will explore seven essential steps to ensure a successful onboarding process.

## 1. Make a Strong First Impression

Onboarding should make new hires feel excited and grateful to join the team. It’s not about perfection, but about showing that the company is high-potential, well-organised, and headed in the right direction. Create an onboarding experience that feels exclusive and well-prepared, conveying that your company is serious and has a clear plan.

## 2. Show Warmth and Appreciation

Make new team members feel welcome and valued. Explain why you chose them and how their unique skills and strengths will contribute to the company’s success. This helps them feel excited and motivated to be part of the team.

## 3. Introduce Team Members

Beyond roles, introduce new hires to the personalities behind the team. Share team members’ professional and personal backgrounds, hobbies, and interests. This humanises the team and fosters early connections, making new hires feel part of the community.

## 4. Share the Mission and Vision

Effectively communicating the company’s mission and vision is essential. Rather than presenting these on a slide, weave them into anecdotes or stories about the company’s goals. For instance, if the company’s mission is to tackle climate change, explain how the team is building a solution for a 100% green grid and why everyone’s role is pivotal in reaching this goal. Make the mission feel real and inspiring.

## 5. Convey Core Values

Introduce your company’s values authentically, backed by real examples. Avoid simply listing values that don’t reflect the day-to-day experience. For instance, if your team prioritises intelligent decision-making over long hours, explain how this translates into practices like work-life balance, rest, and creativity. Ensure that values shared during onboarding reflect what the team lives by.

6. Explain Work Processes and Tools

Help new hires understand how the company operates. Outline the three core tools: 

- **File storage:** Where files are stored and how they’re organised, for example, Google Drive.
- **Project management: **Explain how tasks are tracked, deadlines are managed, and responsibilities are assigned (e.g. ClickUp).
- **Communication: **Clarify the primary communication channel (e.g., Slack) and how it’s used (e.g., using threads for specific topics).

This clarity helps new hires get up to speed quickly and work effectively within your existing systems.

## 7. Define Roles and Responsibilities

Provide new hires with a clear understanding of their role and responsibilities. Although tasks may shift in a startup, set expectations about their primary focus, boundaries, and key metrics. Encourage dialogue to ensure they fully understand and are comfortable with their role. For instance, if they’re hired for sales, make it clear that their focus should be on customer acquisition and that their efforts will drive the company closer to product-market fit.

By following these steps, you’ll create an onboarding experience that’s more thoughtful than most, helping your team align with the company’s mission and goals. This approach will set you up to find product-market fit faster and scale more effectively than other founders.
