# Selling During the Interview

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/cmKhvWuvVZw)

Hiring isn’t just about evaluation — it’s also about **selling the role and the company**. Candidates aren’t only being assessed; they are also assessing you. How you run the process often communicates more than a thousand words on a job description.

## Professionalism as the First Sales Pitch

Every part of your process sends a signal:

- The way you communicate.
- How structured and fast your interviews are.
- Whether your questions challenge them and show real preparation.

Candidates infer your company’s culture and standards from the experience. A well-run process tells them you are serious, competent, and worthy of their time. In many ways, this is already the **strongest sales pitch** you can make.

## Selling Like You Would Sell Anything Else

Beyond professionalism, you need to actively sell the role itself. Think of it as a form of consultative sales:

1. **Understand what the candidate wants.** Do they care about equity? Do they crave impact and mission? Are they motivated by financial upside or career progression?
2. **Position the role as the solution.** Once you know their priorities, you connect the role and your company’s offering directly to those needs.

For example, if someone values financial reward, emphasise the commission structure or bonus potential. If they want ownership, highlight equity and long-term upside. And, if they care about purpose, connect them to the company’s mission and vision.

### Preparing Your Sales Catalogue

The best interviewers don’t improvise. They prepare a “sales catalogue” of arguments in advance:

- **Vision**: Why the mission matters and why now.
- **Compensation**: How the package is structured and competitive.
- **Growth & Learning**: What skills, mentors, and experiences they will gain.
- **Impact**: The unique opportunity to shape the company’s trajectory.

You won’t use every argument. Instead, you selectively deploy the one or two that matter most for that candidate.

### When to Sell

There are natural moments in the process to sell the role:

- At the very start of an interview, to excite and frame the conversation.
- At the very end, when candidates ask questions. This is often the most critical moment — you’ve already learned who they are, and now you can tailor your pitch directly to their values.
