# Introduction

This module is designed to equip you with the knowledge and strategies to successfully raise a dilutive pre-seed or seed funding round for your startup. We'll guide you through developing a fundraising strategy and creating compelling materials like investor decks and data rooms, and mastering best practices for effective outreach to investors.

Getting your fundraising strategy and outreach right can be crucial. According to CB Insights, 28% of startups mentioned running out of cash as one of the reasons for their failure. Another 9% mentioned a lack of investor interest.

![Reasons startups fail according to CB Insights, 2017](/api/media/file/M-L1T1-ReasonsFail.png)

When you raise dilutive funding – the most common route for early-stage startups – you're essentially selling a portion of your company (equity) in exchange for capital to fuel your growth. The price investors are willing to pay for that equity determines your startup's valuation. This process involves issuing new shares, which means you and your co-founders (and other existing shareholders) will experience dilution.

![Your startup basically creates two products: the product your sell to customers and shares you sell to investors.](/api/media/file/VD-AAVF-L1T1%20%E2%80%93%20Two%20products.png)

Your fundraising efforts are therefore a constant tug of war over the following questions that we will help you answer in the coming topics:

1. How much funding should you raise to reach the next milestone?
2. What is a realistic and achievable valuation for your startup at this stage?
3. Which (types of) investors are the best fit for your vision and needs?
4. What fundraising strategy aligns best with your startup and your strengths as founders?
