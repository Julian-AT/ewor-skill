# Launch Posts vs Stealth Startups

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/N6m7VX8-1to)

In this topic, we will look at whether you should go the stealth start-up route or if you should go with a launch post.

Basically, on opposite sides of the spectrum, you should never have a mixture of stealth start-up and launch post. Instead, when deciding between launching your start-up publicly or keeping it stealth, it's crucial to understand the implications.
With a stealth approach, VCs often use scrapers to identify start-ups and potential founders. You can manipulate scrapers by strategically including certain keywords or affiliations in your LinkedIn profile. For instance, adding prestigious university names or prominent company affiliations temporarily can attract attention.
On the other hand, if you opt for a public launch, plan a launch post that is emotional and compelling, sharing why you started the company and what it aims to achieve. To gain traction and visibility, encourage people to like and follow your LinkedIn company page. Leverage personal and employee networks to invite followers, aiming to reach 500 followers in the first day for a strong initial signal. This follower growth is closely monitored by VC scrapers, and a rapid increase can attract inbound traffic and attention from potential investors.

### Writing a Launch Post

When crafting a successful launch post for your start-up, consider the following tips:

1. **Use Visuals**: Visual content, such as images or graphics, tends to receive more engagement on LinkedIn. Incorporate compelling visuals to make your post stand out.
2. **Optimal Posting Time**: Schedule your launch post during optimal posting times, typically between 7:30 and 8:30 am, as well as between 12 and 1 pm. Adjust the timing based on your network's geographic location, with later posting times for a US-centric audience.
3. **Engage Your Network**: Include mentions of people in your network to increase visibility. Encourage these individuals to like, comment, and share your post within the first hour. This initial engagement can boost your post's visibility.
4. **Use Mentions**: If relevant, include links to noteworthy individuals or organisations. Encourage them to engage with your post, providing additional traction.
5. **Leverage EWOR Slack**: Share your launch in the EWOR Slack channel to notify and involve the community. Delete the UTM source from the link before sharing to ensure increased engagement.
6. **Encourage Reshares**: Actively seek reshares of your post within the community. This can amplify the reach and impact of your launch.

Before posting anything on LinkedIn, ensure that your content is proofread and error-free. ChatGPT may not capture your personal tone accurately, so focus on making it sound like you.
Once the post is live, give it your full attention for one day, engaging with comments and leveraging the initial traction. Additionally, share the post with particular people as well, asking them to like, reshare, comment, and write positive feedback. Your post may receive increased attention and interaction because of this manual outreach. If you don't see the desired inbound traffic, consider reaching out for further analysis and advice on how to generate additional engagement.
You can also use the ‘Edit’ section of your LinkedIn profile to link the launch post and make it one of the first things people see when they visit your profile.
