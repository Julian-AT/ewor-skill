# Product Demos

In this topic, we will have a quick look at product demos. But first, we will explain what we think a perfect product demo features.

### Recording Your Video

Make your product demo stand out by going beyond just a screen recording. Consider using tools like Loom, or hiring a professional video editor from sites like Fiverr or Upwork to improve the quality of your demo. This investment, typically between $100 and $200, can be worthwhile if the product demo is an important component of your business.

Furthermore, if you're in the video, make sure to record it with a clear voice. Ensure that you sound confident and are free of interruptions, chaos, and frequent corrections in your sentences. You may need to practise this a few time or re-record. In the end, what matters is that you produce something that is high quality to maintain professionalism and guarantee a lasting impression of your product demo.

**The Demo Contents**

In addition, consider the professionalism of your product demonstration. If your user experience (UX) is unpolished, address it openly in the video. Clearly communicate the stage of development, whether it's a quick and dirty version or you've already made some progress. While competent angels may place less importance on a refined UX, venture capitalists frequently prioritise a more polished presentation. Adapt your approach to your audience, remembering that VCs may place a higher value on a sophisticated presentation than certain angel investors.

Ensure that the flow of your demonstration is consistent, so that viewers can clearly understand your product's functionality. It is critical that people do not become lost in a sea of features. Instead, choose a shorter video that clearly communicates the problem your software addresses and shows how it efficiently solves it with a set number of clicks. To effectively communicate the value proposition of your product, focus on clarity and simplicity.
