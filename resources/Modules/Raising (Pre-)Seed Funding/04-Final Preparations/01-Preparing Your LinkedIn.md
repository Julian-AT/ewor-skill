# Preparing Your LinkedIn

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/oO2-2hdNTxk)

In this topic, we will explore how to optimise your LinkedIn profile to help give you an edge before you engage with investors. 

### A Memorable Headline

The first part of your LinkedIn, that holds a lot of influence, is your headline. Consider tailoring your headline to appeal to investors by incorporating a mission statement, character statement, or a combination that authentically reflects your values. For example, if your target group is VCs and angel investors, you might use a headline like "On a mission to revolutionise customer service with AI" or "Engineer on a mission to revolutionise X, Y, Zed." Additionally, including relevant accolades such as Forbes Under 30 or past associations with notable brands can enhance your profile's appeal in the VC landscape. While authenticity is key, recognizing the superficial nature of the VC game and strategically using such signals can significantly impact their perception of you.

### Your Profile Picture

LinkedIn is a great place for investors to ‘meet you’ before actually meeting you. Therefore, it’s important they not only see what you can do, but who you are.

When selecting a profile picture for your LinkedIn, opt for a professional and pleasant headshot. Ensure that it is clear, without distractions in the background, and portrays a sense of professionalism. You have the flexibility to choose a more casual or formal look, depending on your personal style and the image you want to convey. It's essential that your headshot is well-lit, focused on you, and projects a positive and approachable image. Remember, professionalism is key, whether you choose a more casual or formal attire.

### Tailoring Your Experiences

The next step is to highlight your start-up prominently on your LinkedIn profile, positioning it as the first experience that visitors see. Ensure that, if you have other experiences listed, it is clear that they are part-time or voluntary engagements. This emphasises your full-time commitment to your start-up. Ideally, all other experiences should be dated up to the present day, with your start-up being the sole and current focus. 

- **What to Include Regarding Your Venture: **Moreover, consider including your company logo, a link to your company's website, and a compelling description of what your company does. It's crucial to ensure that the website link is functional. In many cases, a broken URL results in an immediate decline, especially if the LinkedIn profile is part of an application. A non-functional or poorly designed website can be a red flag, indicating that your start-up may not be well-prepared or ready for interaction. If your website is not presentable or if you don’t have one yet, consider labelling your start-up as a "Stealth Startup".
- **Limit the Number of Experiences: **Ensure that your LinkedIn profile highlights only the most relevant experiences. Limit the number of experiences to four or five, focusing on those that are truly impactful and relevant to your professional journey. Avoid cluttering your profile with numerous experiences, as it can be distracting and may give the impression of attempting to artificially inflate your LinkedIn presence. When deciding whether to use descriptions for your experiences, consider the nature of each entry. For ventures you founded, provide a brief description of what the venture does. For other experiences, such as roles at companies like Meta or Google, you can opt to leave the description empty or include a concise statement about your role, such as "Software Engineer." Be consistent in your approach – if you choose to include descriptions, do so for all non-venture experiences, and if you prefer to have no descriptions, maintain that consistency across your profile.
- **Education: **In the education section of your LinkedIn profile, strategically showcase your academic background to provide VCs with a clear understanding of your expertise. If you have a technical background, emphasise your GPA, the specific technical subject of your degree, and any relevant academic achievements. For business-oriented individuals, focus on extracurricular activities during your education, such as clubs or specific projects, especially if you pursued an MBA. Arrange your educational experiences in descending order of importance so that the most significant ones are highlighted first. Avoid prioritising weak certificates or online courses at the top of your education section, especially if they don't hold the same weight as traditional academic degrees. If you didn't attend a prestigious institution like Harvard, refrain from placing online certifications at the forefront of your profile, as it might come across as an attempt to associate yourself with such institutions. Be truthful and prioritise the educational experiences that truly reflect your knowledge and expertise, as savvy investors will delve deeper to uncover the details.
- **Media and Links: **The next thing you can do is enhance your LinkedIn profile by adding media and links to showcase your experiences. If you have received significant press coverage or have been featured in notable publications such as Sifted or Forbes, ensure that you include these accomplishments in the media section. Highlighting such achievements will provide a tangible demonstration of your expertise and accomplishments, making your profile more compelling for potential investors. Review any previous posts on LinkedIn and ensure that they align with your start-up and present an authentic representation of your interests and expertise. If you find posts that are unrelated or don't resonate with your start-up's focus, consider creating new posts that showcase your competence in relevant areas. Craft posts with viral potential that highlight your expertise, especially in subjects related to your start-up. Engage with the community to amplify the reach of these posts, demonstrating to VCs that you are not only a knowledgeable but also a likeable individual.
- **Responsiveness: **During the fundraising period, prioritise responsiveness when dealing with VCs. While it can be strategic to be less responsive during the pre-fundraising phase, once you officially enter the fundraising process, ensure that you promptly respond to messages and inquiries from VCs. Demonstrating quick and attentive communication during this stage is generally seen as a positive sign and can contribute to building a favourable relationship with potential investors. After creating your LinkedIn profile, review it to ensure authenticity and professionalism. If you're satisfied with how it represents you, include it in your email footer and investor communications. Last but not least, ensure that your team members also maintain clean and professional LinkedIn profiles. Having a cohesive and polished online presence for everyone in the start-up can positively contribute to your image when interacting with potential investors.
