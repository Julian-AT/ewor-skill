# Product Demos

Providing investors with a product demo allows them to directly experience your solution and see firsthand how it addresses the problem you've identified. Below are some key considerations for recording your product demo.

## Recording Your Video

**Show How You Solve the Problem**

The demo's core purpose is to clearly illustrate how your product alleviates the pain points highlighted in your “Problem Slide”. Focus the narrative of the demo around the problem-solution fit.

**Keep it Concise and Clear**

Respect investors' time. Aim for a shorter video that focuses on the most critical functionality. Avoid getting lost in a multitude of features; instead, show how a set number of clicks or steps efficiently solves the problem.

**Showcase the User Experience (UX)**

Demonstrate the flow and ease of use of your product. If your UX is still unpolished in early development, address this openly and transparently in the video, clearly stating the current stage of development. Be mindful that VCs often prioritise a more polished presentation than some angels, so tailor your approach to your audience.

**Ensure High-Quality Production**

Invest in clear audio and stable video recording. Whether using tools like Loom or hiring a professional video editor from platforms like Fiverr or Upwork, aim for a high-quality output. Record with a clear, confident voice, free from interruptions or excessive corrections. This professionalism leaves a positive lasting impression.
