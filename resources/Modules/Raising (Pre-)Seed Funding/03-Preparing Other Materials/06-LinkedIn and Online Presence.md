# LinkedIn and Online Presence

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/oO2-2hdNTxk)

Your online presence, particularly your LinkedIn profile, plays a significant role in fundraising and customer acquisition. While your direct interactions (in-person or video calls) and email communication are crucial, investors also conduct informal due diligence by reviewing your online presence, such as your LinkedIn profile, company website, and any press mentions. This often happens before they look at your formal data room. What they find can significantly influence their decision and may even be discussed with other investors. Therefore, it's essential to manage your online narrative proactively and keep your information up to date. Google yourself, scrutinise what's publicly available, and ensure it aligns with the story you want to tell in your pitch.

## Optimising Your LinkedIn Profile

Turn your LinkedIn profile into a valuable asset for fundraising and networking. Here's how:

- **Craft a Memorable Headline:** Your headline is highly influential. Tailor it to resonate with investors by incorporating a concise mission or character statement that authentically reflects your values and startup's focus (e.g., “Revolutionising customer service with AI” or “Engineers building the future of X”). Include relevant accolades like industry awards or associations with notable brands if applicable, as these can strategically enhance your profile's appeal to VCs.
- **Choose a Professional Profile Picture:** Your profile picture is often an investor's first visual impression. Select a professional, clear, and pleasant headshot. Ensure the background is free of distractions and the lighting is good. While you can adapt the style (casual or formal) to your personal brand, professionalism is key.
- **Tailor Your Experience Section:** Prominently feature your startup as your primary and current experience, ideally at the top of your profile. Clearly indicate if other listed experiences are part-time or voluntary to emphasize your full-time commitment to your venture. Ensure all other experiences are dated correctly up to the present.
- **Highlight Your Venture or stay in stealth mode:** Include your company logo, a functional link to your company's website, and a compelling, concise description of what your startup does. A broken website link or a poorly designed site can be a significant red flag, signalling a lack of preparedness. If your website isn't ready, consider labelling your startup as a "Stealth Startup." Limit your listed experiences to the most impactful and relevant four or five to avoid clutter.
- **Describe Relevant Experiences:** For ventures you've founded, provide a brief description. For other roles (e.g., at larger companies), you can keep descriptions brief (“Software Engineer”) or leave them empty, but maintain consistency throughout your profile.
- **Showcase Your Education:** Use the education section to clearly convey your academic background and relevant expertise. For technical degrees, highlight your subject, GPA, and academic achievements. For business backgrounds (including MBA's), emphasise extracurricular activities, clubs, or relevant projects. Prioritise your most significant educational experiences. Avoid placing less weighty certificates or online courses at the top if they don't align with your primary academic achievements. Be truthful; savvy investors will verify details.
- **Add Media and Links:** Enhance your profile by adding media or links that showcase relevant experiences and accomplishments. Include press coverage from notable publications or any other tangible proof of your expertise.
- **Curate Your Activity:** Review your past LinkedIn posts to ensure they align with your startup's narrative and present a professional image. Consider creating new posts that highlight your knowledge and expertise in your field, ideally with the potential to engage your network and amplify your reach.

## Your Team's Online Presence

Finally, remember that your team's online presence also contributes to your overall image. Encourage your co-founders and key team members to maintain clean, professional, and up-to-date LinkedIn profiles that collectively reinforce the startup's narrative.
