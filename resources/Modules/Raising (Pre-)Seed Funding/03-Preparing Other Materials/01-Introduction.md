# Introduction

Investors want strong conviction that the expected return on their investment in your startup is high. Your investor deck plays a crucial role in building this confidence, but many (professional) investors will request additional materials that provide deeper insights into your business.

In the topics below, we will delve into the following essential materials you should prepare:

## **Legal Documents**

- Proof of Incorporation & Registration: Demonstrating that your company has a solid legal foundation is crucial for investor trust.
- Detailed Cap Table: Investors need to understand your current ownership structure and the impact of their investment.
- Co-Founder Agreement or Shareholder Agreement:** **This document outlines the roles, responsibilities, equity split, and decision-making processes among the founders and early shareholders, which is vital for investors to understand the stability and governance of your company.
- Intellectual Property Documentation: If your startup has patents, trademarks, or other IP, having this documentation readily available is important.

## **FAQ & FYI**

- **Financial Model: **Providing a detailed financial model allows investors to understand your projections and assess your financial planning.
- **Product Demos: **A live or recorded demo showcases your product's functionality and user experience.
- **Strong LinkedIn Profiles and Online Presence: Y**our online presence reflects your professionalism and the visibility of your team and startup.
