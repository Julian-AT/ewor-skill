# FAQs and FYI

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/6OBV8HSbp4c)

Beyond your pitch and deck, preparing two key documents can significantly enhance your performance during investor calls: a Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) list and a For Your Information (FYI) document.

## **The FAQ Document**

A classic FAQ is a comprehensive list of questions commonly asked by investors, complete with your concise and well-thought-out answers. To build this document, start by:

- **The Problem:** Prepare to go beyond your slide by documenting rich, detailed anecdotes and customer stories that bring the problem you are solving to life.
- **Defensibility:** To show what makes your solution unique, prepare a "killer argument" by documenting evidence of your innovative technology, a patent, a first-mover advantage, or your team's unique expertise.
- **Target Market:** Prepare to demonstrate a thorough understanding of your target markets. Document your analysis in a professional spreadsheet or have all the key details memorised.
- **Business Model:** Investors will look for concrete evidence of your business model's viability. Prepare to back up your claims with tangible proof, such as benchmarks from similar companies or a convincing analogy. Document stories from customer discovery that prove people will pay for your solution.
- **Risks:** Acknowledge the challenges your business faces and prepare a clear strategy for how you plan to mitigate them. Being candid about risks shows that you are aware of potential problems and have a plan to address them, which builds investor confidence.
- **Scaling:** Prepare to discuss both technological and organisational scalability. Document how your solution can handle increased demand without a proportional increase in workload, and outline a clear plan for how your team and processes will adapt to growth.

Whenever you encounter a question in a call that you weren't ready for, be sure to note it down and immediately add it to your FAQ. This iterative process ensures you are always prepared for future conversations.

## **The FYI Document**

The FYI document is a collection of compelling anecdotes and stories related to each slide of your investor deck. These are not standard data points; they are the human-interest stories that add depth and excitement to your pitch. Examples include:

- An anecdote about a talented individual who took a significant pay cut to join your team because they believe in the mission.
- A story about a loyal customer who was so impressed with your product they quit their job to become an employee.

During investor calls, subtly weave these anecdotes into your conversation. While you won't use every story in every meeting, having a list of around 20 well-prepared, authentic stories gives you a powerful tool to captivate and convince investors, moving beyond just the numbers and highlighting the unique narrative of your company.
