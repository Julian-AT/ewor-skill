# The Market Size Slide

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BDK1RGsM_iI)

When crafting your market slide, your primary goal is to convince investors that the opportunity is massive and ripe for growth, aligning with their pursuit of high-return ventures.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

Clearly define and accurately calculate your market opportunity by focusing on:

- Total Addressable Market (TAM): Showcase the entire potential market for your solution, illustrating the overall scope of the opportunity.
- Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): Narrow this down to the portion of the TAM that your product or service can realistically target. Crucially, your SAM needs to demonstrate the potential to become a €100 million Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) company.
- Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM): This crucial segment represents the market you can realistically capture in the very near future.

To validate these market sizes:

- Calculate Your Market Using Both Top-Down and Bottom-Up Approaches: Employ both macro market data and analysis of your potential customer base to validate your TAM, SAM, and SOM estimations. Always back up your claims with credible data and clearly cite your sources.
- Highlight Your Unique Market Niche: Clearly demonstrate that your venture occupies a distinct and advantageous position within the broader market.
- Keep it Concise and Visual: Present your market size data in a clear and easily digestible format using concise language and impactful visuals like charts and graphs to illustrate market size, growth trends, and your projected market share.

## Common Mistakes:

- Incorrectly Defining Your Market: Avoid grossly overestimating your market size by confusing it with a related but much larger market. For example, if you have restaurant software, your market isn't the total restaurant revenue, but the existing (or expected) spending on such software.
- Overlooking the Impact of Pricing: Failing to consider how your pricing strategy affects your realistic market size can lead to inflated estimates. A significantly cheaper product, while potentially appealing to a broader audience, will have a proportionally smaller revenue potential for the same market share.
- Assuming Simplistic Market Share: Avoid taking an arbitrary small percentage of a huge market without a clear strategy for capturing it. Even a small slice of a large pie requires significant effort and a well-defined approach in a competitive landscape.
- Confusing Problems with Markets: Identifying broad societal issues as your market overlooks the distinct segments within them, each with its own dynamics and players.
- Underestimating VC Expectations: Remember the $100 million+ ARR target. Your market needs to support this kind of growth - if not now, than in the near future.
- Focusing on Obvious Market Characteristics: Don't spend excessive time convincing investors of the size of well-known markets. Instead, focus on the strategic advantages of your specific niche and your plan for disruption.

### Market Size Slide Examples

### Revolut

![Market Size Slide from Revolut’s 2015 Seed Pitch Deck](/api/media/file/market%20size)

Revolut, in their 2015 Seed Pitch Deck, effectively demonstrated how to **'Show the Sheer Size and Potential of the Opportunity'**. They achieved this by highlighting impressive statistics such as the billions lost due to traditional banking spreads and fees, alongside the trillions in global transactional value, immediately underscoring the vast market they were targeting.
