# The Financial Projections Slide

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/y9aPPeCvfN8)

While typically less crucial in the very early pre-seed stages due to inherent uncertainties, the Financial Projections slide becomes increasingly important as you progress, particularly by the Series A stage. This slide aims to give investors a sense of your future financial potential and demonstrate your understanding of key financial metrics and business drivers.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- **Focus on Key Metrics in a Well-Structured Table:** Present a clear and concise table including essential metrics such as projected headcount, customer numbers, revenue (and ARR/MRR if applicable to your business model), burn rate, cash balance, required capital for the projected period, and major product or business milestones. Project these figures for the next three years using a timeframe (monthly, quarterly, or yearly) that best reflects your business growth pattern.
- **Include Historical Data and Current Performance (If Available):** Incorporating past performance data and your current financial standing provides crucial context and strengthens the credibility of your projections, especially if you have a few years of operational history (e.g., by Series A).
- **Be Realistic and Data-Driven:** While some level of forecasting involves assumptions, strive to base your projections on reasonable and well-justified assumptions. Where possible, especially in more predictable industries like e-commerce or hardware manufacturing, benchmark against available industry data or comparable companies.
- **Highlight Key Underlying Assumptions:** Briefly outline the most important assumptions that underpin your financial projections. This shows investors your understanding of the key factors influencing your future performance.
- **Align with Milestones:** Ensure a clear alignment between your financial projections and the major product development, market expansion, or other significant milestones you've outlined in your roadmap.

## Common Mistakes:

- **Including Too Early (Pre-Seed):** Generally, detailed financial projections are less critical at the pre-seed stage, where fundamental uncertainties are high. Focus instead on demonstrating traction and strong unit economics. However, some seasoned angel investors or specific institutional investors might still expect a high-level overview.
- **Speculative Forecasts Without Foundation:** Avoid presenting overly optimistic or purely speculative long-term financial forecasts, especially in the very early stages where you lack substantial historical data.
- **Idealistic and Unsubstantiated Numbers:** Ensure your projections are grounded in realistic assumptions rather than wishful thinking. Avoid claiming perpetual free services funded solely by less reliable revenue streams like advertising in a competitive market.
- **Lack of Connection to Operational Plans:** Your financial projections should logically flow from your go-to-market strategy, product roadmap, and team growth plans. Ensure these elements are consistent.
- **Not Justifying Assumptions:** Failing to clearly articulate the basis for your key assumptions can undermine the credibility of your financial projections. Be prepared to explain the reasoning behind your forecasts.
