# The Traction and Milestones Slide

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8a8qY1PbAwI)

The Traction and Milestones slide often emerges as the pivotal moment for investors, providing crucial evidence for the **probability of success** component of their expected value calculation. This slide serves as the decisive factor in demonstrating that your startup has moved beyond just an idea and is successfully gaining traction in the market, thereby reducing investment risk.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- **Show Exponential Growth (If Applicable):** If you've achieved rapid acceleration, particularly in paying customers or key user metrics, showcase this visually with clear charts illustrating a sharp upward trajectory over time. Even seemingly small numbers can be compelling when presented as exponential growth.
- **Highlight Key Traction Metrics Relevant to Your Business:**
- - **For B2C:** Emphasize user count, user growth rate, user retention (churn rate), revenue generated, and revenue growth rate. If applicable, mention progress towards profitability.
- **For B2B:** Focus on the number of letters of intent (LOIs), current revenue, customer usage patterns, and the potential for upselling and cross-selling within your existing customer base.
- **Incorporate Social Proof:** Include customer logos of recognizable brands, compelling testimonials from satisfied users, and a visual representation of your customer acquisition funnel to provide additional validation of your progress.
- **Showcase Key Milestones Achieved and Planned:** Outline the significant milestones you've reached since your last funding round (if any) and clearly present the key milestones you plan to achieve with the new investment. Organize these milestones in a coherent timeline to illustrate your progress and future roadmap.
- **Get Creative with Traction if Pre-Revenue:** If you haven't yet generated revenue, highlight other compelling indicators of traction, such as a significant waitlist volume that represents potential future revenue, or strong engagement metrics with your product.
- **Focus on Absolute Numbers if Growth is Linear (but Significant):** If exponential growth isn't yet evident, emphasize significant absolute numbers, such as a substantial current customer base, to demonstrate existing market validation.

## Common Mistakes:

- **Showcasing Linearity Without Strong Absolute Numbers:** Presenting only gradual, linear growth without a compelling current customer base often fails to excite investors. In such cases, focus on the absolute numbers you have achieved.
- **Highlighting Irrelevant Metrics or Milestones:** Ensure that all metrics and milestones presented directly demonstrate the traction and progress of your business. Avoid including peripheral achievements like office setups or general team growth that aren't tied to product adoption or revenue.
- **Confusing Market Size with Traction:** Discussing the total number of users in your target industry is not traction; focus on users specifically engaging with or signing up for *your* product.
- **Lack of Clear Focus:** Ensure the slide clearly highlights your most impressive traction metrics and relevant milestones, avoiding clutter or overwhelming investors with too much information. If you lack significant traction, consider omitting the slide altogether rather than showcasing weak or irrelevant data.
