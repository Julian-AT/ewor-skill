# The Mission and Vision Slide

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/i299qqVcQsY)

The Mission & Vision slide, often the very first in your investor deck, serves to succinctly express your startup's fundamental objectives and long-term aspirations. Its primary purpose is to paint a compelling picture of where you're headed and why it matters.

### Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

This slide is your opportunity to inspire investors with your ambition. To make it impactful and memorable, focus on the following:

- Communicate an Audacious Goal with Impactful Language: Investors, particularly VCs, are looking for significant potential returns. Your vision should reflect a bold and transformative impact you aim to achieve in the future, ideally within the next decade. Use strong and inspiring language to convey the scale of your ambition. Some investors might even pass if the vision isn't audacious enough.
- Clearly and Concisely Articulate What You Do: Ensure your mission and vision statements clearly convey the core activity of your startup in simple, memorable terms. Investors should immediately grasp what problem you're solving or what future you're building.
- Explain Your "Why" and Align with Your Narrative: Briefly touch upon the underlying motivation and passion driving your company's founding. This personal element can create an emotional connection with investors and should align with the overall story you're telling in your pitch deck.
- Hint at the "Why Now?" and Focus on the Future: Subtly suggest the timeliness and urgency of your vision. Why is now the right moment for your company to exist and pursue this mission? Keep your focus on the future impact you intend to create.
- Consider Visuals: A compelling image or graphic can often enhance the impact and memorability of your mission and vision.

### Common Mistakes:

- Referencing Past Achievements Instead of Future Aspirations: Avoid focusing on what established companies have already accomplished. Your mission should project originality and innovation, outlining your unique future impact. Investors want to see what *you* will achieve.
- Creating a Mission That is Too Narrow or Small: If your mission doesn't suggest the potential for significant scale and market disruption, it may not align with the high-growth expectations of venture capital. Ensure your ambition is large enough to be relevant to the world of VC.
- Lacking Clarity: If your mission and vision are ambiguous or unclear, investors will struggle to understand your core objectives. This can raise doubts about your ability to articulate your goals to employees, advisors, customers, and other stakeholders, hindering your ability to attract both talent and customers.
- Overcrowding the Slide: Keep your mission and vision statements brief and easy to understand at a glance. Avoid dense paragraphs of text.

### Mission and Vision Examples

#### SIMBA

![Source: TechCrunch](/api/media/file/simba)

Consider SIMBA’s Mission & Vision Slide, which was part of the investor deck with which they raised around $25 million in 2022. Their mission, 'We are the picks and shovels of the blockchain rush,' is a prime example of clarity and impact. This analogy immediately communicates their role in providing essential infrastructure for the growing blockchain ecosystem, similar to Stripe's role in online payments. 

#### Arkive

![Source: TechCrunch](/api/media/file/arkive)

Another example is Arkive, which successfully raised approximately $9.7 million. Their vision slide began with the thought-provoking question: 'What if the Smithsonian was owned and curated by the Internet?'. This instantly presented an audacious vision of democratizing cultural institutions, effectively challenging the status quo and prompting investors to envision a decentralized future.

#### Bikeflip

![Source: TechCrunch](/api/media/file/bikeflip)

Finally, consider Bikeflip's vision slide: 'The Autoscout24 of the bicycle world.' This concise statement effectively leverages the power of analogy, immediately conveying their platform's purpose by referencing a well-known and successful model in the automotive industry. This clear vision highlights an unmet need in the bicycle market and articulates a broader ambition to make the world more efficient through its platform, showcasing both uniqueness and potential impact.
