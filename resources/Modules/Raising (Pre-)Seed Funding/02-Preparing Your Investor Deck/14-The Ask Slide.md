# The Ask Slide

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UE7uLAtQZpY)

The Ask slide is a fundamental component of your investor deck where you clearly communicate your funding needs and how that capital will fuel your startup's progress. It directly addresses both the probability of success (by outlining how you'll use the funds to achieve milestones) and the potential return (by showcasing how this investment will drive growth and future valuation).

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- **Clearly State Your Ask:** Be upfront and specific about the exact amount of funding you are seeking.
- **Explain Your Strategic Use of Funds:** Detail precisely how you plan to allocate the raised capital across key areas such as team expansion, product development, marketing and sales initiatives, technology acquisition, or operational expenses. This demonstrates thoughtful planning.
- **Outline Key Milestones the Funding Will Enable:** Go beyond simply listing expenditures. Clearly articulate the specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) milestones that this investment will allow you to accomplish. For example, you might aim to achieve a certain level of user acquisition, launch a key product feature, or expand into a new market.
- **Provide Context with Previous Funding (If Applicable):** If you've successfully raised funds in the past, briefly mention the amount raised and the valuation at that stage. Transparency here builds trust and provides valuable context for potential investors.
- **Consider Presenting a Strategic Funding Range:** It's acceptable to present a funding range (e.g., "$500k to $2.5 million") if you clearly outline the specific goals and outcomes associated with each level of investment. This demonstrates strategic thinking and flexibility. For instance, explain what key milestones you can achieve with the lower amount and how a larger investment would enable accelerated growth or expansion into new areas.
- **Lean Towards a Realistic Initial Ask:** While a range is acceptable, consider leaning towards the lower end of your funding spectrum for your initial ask. This can provide flexibility and may be perceived more favourably by investors.

## Common Mistakes:

- **Excluding This Crucial Slide:** Always include an "Ask" slide in your investor deck.
- **Being Vague About Your Needs:** Avoid ambiguity regarding the specific amount of funding you require and how it will be utilized. Lack of clarity undermines investor confidence.
- **Omitting Key Goals and Milestones:** Don't just detail where the money will go; clearly articulate the specific, impactful milestones you intend to achieve with the investment. This helps investors understand the potential return on their capital.
- **Asking for an Unjustified Amount:** Ensure your funding request is reasonable and directly tied to your outlined milestones and growth strategy. Avoid asking for an excessive amount without a clear plan for its effective use.
- **Not Connecting Funding to Future Success:** Clearly articulate how the requested capital will contribute to achieving profitability, setting the stage for subsequent funding rounds, or ultimately leading to a successful exit.
