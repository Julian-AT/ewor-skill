# The Business Model Slide

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=55MWsgs6Rno)

The aim of your business model slide is to clearly explain to investors how your company will generate revenue and ultimately achieve profitability. Even in the early stages, outlining a well-thought-out revenue plan is crucial, especially during a seed round. Remember that this plan can evolve, but it's essential to demonstrate that you've considered how you'll make money and become sustainable.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- **Clearly Explain Your Pricing Strategy:** Detail how you will price your product or service. Mention different pricing tiers, subscription levels, or usage-based models if applicable.
- **Outline Your Distribution Strategies:** Explain the primary ways you will get your product or service into the hands of your customers. This could include direct sales, online channels, partnerships, or a combination.
- **Identify Your Business Model Type:** Clearly state the type of business model you are employing (e.g., subscription, freemium, marketplace, SaaS, e-commerce). If it's a well-known model, simply naming it might suffice. If it's more complex or less common, provide a brief, clear explanation of how it works.
- **Showcase Strong Unit Economics (If Applicable):** If your business model demonstrates healthy unit economics, such as a Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost (LTV:CAC) ratio of 3:1 or higher (ideally much higher), highlight this as it provides strong evidence of your financial viability and scalability.
- **Focus on Revenue Generation:** Clearly articulate the primary ways your company will generate revenue. Be specific about your revenue streams.
- **Provide Evidence of Willingness to Pay (If Possible):** If you have data from market research, pilot programs, or early sales that supports your chosen pricing model, include it to bolster your case.

## Common Mistakes:

- **Overcomplicating the Explanation:** Avoid overwhelming investors with a full business model canvas or overly intricate diagrams. Focus on clearly explaining how you generate revenue.
- **Unfavourable LTV:CAC:** Be cautious if your pricing model suggests that achieving profitability will be a struggle (especially if your LTV:CAC ratio is below 3:1). If this is the case, be prepared to articulate a clear plan for improvement.
- **Idealistic Projections:** Avoid overly optimistic or unrealistic projections, such as assuming perpetual free services funded solely by advertising or user data sales. Focus on a business model that aligns with the current market landscape and your company's realistic growth trajectory.
- **Lack of a Concrete Plan:** Even if you anticipate significant future growth without a detailed monetization strategy (like early-stage social networks), acknowledge this while emphasizing your potential. However, for most startups, demonstrating a clear path to revenue is expected, especially by the seed stage.
