# The Competition Slide

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CYZdV9UB-ZQ)

The primary goal of your competition slide is to convince investors that existing solutions inadequately address the problem you're solving and to clearly demonstrate your unique position within the competitive landscape.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- **Acknowledge and Clearly Identify Your Competitors:** Demonstrate a thorough understanding of the competitive landscape by identifying both direct and indirect competitors. List several key players, avoiding the common mistake of claiming no competition or omitting obvious contenders.
- **Illustrate Your Differentiation Clearly:** Articulate what sets you apart from your competitors in a way that resonates with customers. Utilize one of the following methods to highlight your competitive advantage:
- - **Competitor Matrix:** Position yourself favourably in the top right quadrant using two key axes that are genuinely important to customers (e.g., scalability vs. quality, price vs. features). Provide evidence, such as customer testimonials, to validate your superior positioning. Avoid “right corner syndrome” by choosing relevant and meaningful axes.

![A competitor matrix compares your venture to other ventures on two axis.](/api/media/file/vd-msc-competitive-analysis-positioning-map.png)

- - **Comparative Table:** Create a table outlining around five key features or dimensions that are crucial to your target customers and compare your offering against those of your main competitors. Emphasize why these dimensions matter to customers and how your startup excels in each.

![Compare your venture to other ventures on multiple axes](/api/media/file/vd-msc-competitive-analysis-matrix.png)

- - **Direct Comparison:** Select two to three key competitors and clearly list the most significant differentiators between your product/service and theirs, focusing on tangible benefits for the customer. Avoid overly technical language and concentrate on high-level advantages.

![Compare your venture to a few main competitors](/api/media/file/vd-aavf-l3-direct-comparison.png)

- **Focus on Meaningful Differences:** Highlight the differentiators that directly address the problem you are solving and where the competition falls short. Avoid focusing on minor or irrelevant differences.
- **Keep it Concise and Benefit-Oriented:** Regardless of the method you choose, present your competitive analysis in a clear and easily digestible format. Focus on the tangible benefits your unique position offers to customers.

## Common Mistakes:

- Lack of Competitor Awareness: Failing to acknowledge well-known competitors or understating their significance can signal a lack of industry knowledge.
- Irrelevant Labels and “Right Corner Syndrome”: Using meaningless labels in matrices or tables that always position you as superior without genuine justification.
- Focusing on Unimportant Differences: Highlighting trivial distinctions that don't actually matter to your target customers.
- Feature Saturation: Including too many features or dimensions in comparison tables, which can overwhelm investors and obscure the key differentiators.
- Overly Technical Language: Using jargon or technical details that investors unfamiliar with your industry won't understand. Keep the focus on high-level benefits.
