# The Product Slide

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/6nNi_Ixma2g)

Having articulated the problem and your strategic solution, the Product slide serves to demonstrate that your offering is the optimal way to execute that solution. Think of the solution as the 'what' and the product as the specific 'how' that makes it tangible and effective.

## Key Elements of a Successful Slide:

- Show, Don't Just Tell (Visuals are Key): Use clear visuals like screenshots, mockups, wireframes, or even a short demo (if feasible) to bring your product to life. As seen with Party Round, Huzzle, Simple Club, and Helu, visuals make your product tangible and easier to understand.
- Focus on Core Functionality and Value: Clearly demonstrate the essential functions of your product and, more importantly, the core value it delivers to your target users. Party Round highlighted simplified fundraising, Huzzle focused on being a career companion, Simple Club emphasized high-quality learning, and Helu showcased simplified financial reporting.
- Keep it Simple and Clear: Aim for a clean and uncluttered presentation. Condense the essential information onto a single slide, or at most two for complex deep tech products. Party Round's slide exemplifies this simplicity.
- Show the Essentials, Not Every Feature: Focus on the holistic value and the key components or steps involved in using your product. Huzzle effectively broke down their product into goal selection, matchmaking, and execution.
- Highlight Your Unique Differentiation: Subtly showcase what makes your product stand out from the competition. Helu highlighted its intuitive user interface as a differentiator from traditional Excel.
- Lead with Core Value: Immediately convey the primary benefit your product offers to the user.
- If Applicable, Briefly Back Up Claims: If you make bold statements about your product's superiority (like Simple Club's content), be prepared to briefly hint at the evidence or the "how" behind it (e.g., their content creation process).

## Common Mistakes:

- Overloading With Features: Resist the temptation to list every single feature your product offers. Focus on the core functionality that directly addresses the problem.
- Missing the Holistic Value: Ensure you communicate the overall impact and synergy of your product's features, rather than just presenting a fragmented list.
- Lack of Visuals: Relying solely on text to describe your product can make it abstract and difficult for investors to grasp.
- Technical Overload: Avoid diving too deep into intricate technical details that might not be relevant at this stage. Focus on the user experience and the value proposition.

## Product Slide Examples

### Party Round

![Source: TechCrunch](/api/media/file/party%20round)

Party Round's product slide, which contributed to their $7 million pre-seed raise, effectively communicates their solution – a user-friendly app for fundraising. Featuring a clear app image and outlining the simplified process of creating a funding round and inviting investors (likened to sending money on a phone), it exemplifies 'Show, Don't Just Tell' and 'Keep it Simple and Clear'. By focusing on the core process and inviting questions on complex legal aspects rather than detailing them on the slide, Party Round avoided the common mistake of 'Overloading With Features', ensuring a clear understanding of their direction and innovation.

### Huzzle

![Source: Huzzle’s Pre-Seed Pitch Deck](/api/media/file/huzzle)

Huzzle's product slide, praised for its exceptional clarity, effectively conveys their product as a 'career companion' – a European counterpart to Handshake. By using screenshots, they 'Show, Don't Just Tell' the ease of goal selection, matchmaking, and the application process, clearly highlighting the 'Core Functionality and Value'. Even though it features three screens, each highlights a crucial functionality, demonstrating 'Show the Essentials' without overwhelming the audience. This approach underscores the simplicity and user-friendliness of their platform.

### Simpleclub

![Source: Simpleclub](/api/media/file/simple%20club)

Simpleclub's product slide, from a company boasting millions of learners and established as a premier education product company in Germany, effectively blends their solution and product. By boldly claiming to have the best educational content and visually showcasing their key features and functionalities – including their web platform, dedicated app, and diverse range of subjects – they demonstrate 'Focus on Core Functionality and Value' and 'Show, Don't Just Tell'. The slide's neat organization makes it easy to grasp the depth and breadth of their offering. Notably, while making the impressive claim of having the best content, Simpleclub also subtly introduces their 'content machine' as a testament to their commitment and as proof to support their daring assertion, highlighting the importance of backing up strong claims with evidence.

### Helu

![Source: TechCrunch](/api/media/file/helu)

Helu, an Austrian company that secured an impressive $10 million investment, effectively conveyed the simplicity and convenience of their solution for automated financial reporting for SMEs by claiming to replace Excel. Their product slide stood out by presenting clear and upgraded wireframes, demonstrating 'Show, Don't Just Tell'. This visually appealing user interface, with subtle pop-ups instilling trust, effectively addressed potential doubts and communicated the 'Unique Differentiation' and excellence of their product.
