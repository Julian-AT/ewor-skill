# Securing Your Term Sheet

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/UroYTYvqNg0)

Closing your funding round typically involves two key steps. First, you need to secure your first term sheet. This initial term sheet often acts as a catalyst, expediting the process. Once you've received this crucial document, things tend to move swiftly. The second step is finalising and officially closing the round. This entails selecting the most favourable offer, signing the necessary documents, and ultimately concluding the transaction. Now let’s have a closer look:

## Securing Your First Term Sheet

When it comes to securing your first term sheet, our first piece of advice is to go the extra mile by:

- **Taking Initiative: **Consider a scenario where a founder demonstrated remarkable initiative. Despite facing initial reluctance from a VC who claimed to be too busy, the founder took bold action by flying all the way to San Francisco for a one-day meeting. This proactive approach convinced the VC to agree to a meeting, leading to the first secured term sheet, paving the way for 15 additional offers. Without this initial term sheet, the outcome might have been different. 
- **Emphasising Personal Connections**: Additionally, emphasise the importance of personal connections. Regular phone calls, seeking advice, and arranging face-to-face meetings can significantly enhance rapport. This personal bonding is especially crucial when securing your first term sheet, as being the first investor can be daunting for many. Building a strong personal relationship fosters trust and confidence, essential elements for securing investment. Particularly with reputable VCs capable of substantial investments and boasting strong brand recognition, investing time and effort to establish personal connections is highly recommended. However, it's essential to respect boundaries; if a VC indicates a lack of interest in further contact, avoid excessive persistence, as it may come across as needy. Nonetheless, if the opportunity arises and the VC is receptive, investing in building a personal relationship can yield invaluable benefits.
- **Creating Traction Awareness: **Lastly, ensure that everyone is aware of your considerable traction. An exemplary founder we know executed this strategy flawlessly. Without appearing arrogant or dishonest, he simply communicated his overwhelming schedule. He'd politely apologise, explaining he had a packed calendar with 70 calls lined up over the next two weeks. However, he assured them he'd follow up later in the evening, dedicated to delivering everything they needed. By subtly dropping these hints, he conveyed that numerous parties found him intriguing. 
In short, we highly recommend translating your traction into compelling anecdotes and conveying them modestly. By doing so, you can significantly increase your chances of securing your first term sheet.

## Negotiating the Terms

After securing your first term sheet, the negotiation process begins, typically starting with discussions on aspects like valuation and liquidity preferences, among other standard terms. If you haven't already, we highly recommend watching the content in our legal course on [term sheets](https://platform.ewor.com/courses/462/lessons/830/?course_id=462) and utilising the [term sheet cheat sheet](https://platform.ewor.com/resource/term-sheet/) available on the EWOR Platform for a simplified understanding of these terms.

During this phase, it's crucial to inform other potential investors that you've received an offer but are not entirely satisfied with it. By signalling your openness to better offers, you create a sense of urgency among investors, encouraging them to improve their offers. Keep iterating this process until you receive the highest and most favourable offer available.

However, it's essential to consider factors beyond just the offer itself. A VC’s ability to provide long-term support and the terms they offer, such as control provisions, are equally important. Take the case of eBay, for example. Despite having an offer with a higher valuation from another investor, eBay opted to raise their first investment from Benchmark at a lower valuation of $20 million pre-money. This decision proved to be pivotal for eBay's success, as Benchmark provided invaluable support, including identifying Meg Whitman as their CEO, contributing to eBay's growth into a billion-dollar company.

Once you've gathered offers from multiple investors, focus on negotiating with the two or three VCs you favour the most. Use your leverage from multiple offers to negotiate terms that align with your preferences.
