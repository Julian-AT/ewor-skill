## **Weekly Updates**

Our first recommendation is to write weekly updates. Dedicate an hour or two each week to compile progress reports on your product, exciting developments in your business, and perhaps a couple of fundraising highlights. Distribute these updates to everyone you're in contact with on a weekly basis. If progress is slower than expected, extend this practice for two to three months. Even if you initially only secure an angel round and face rejections from VCs, don't lose heart. Continuing to send regular updates, perhaps on a monthly basis, can significantly impact your fundraising efforts in the future.  Demonstrating consistent progress and momentum through these updates over the next 12 months can capture investor attention. Many founders have found success with this approach, as it underscores your commitment and momentum to potential investors.

## **Make Noise on Social Media**

** **Number two, leverage social media to amplify your presence. Investors are driven by FOMO (fear of missing out), and a vibrant social media presence can fuel this sentiment. Consider timing your celebratory announcements strategically, perhaps saving some for when you kick off your fundraising campaign. This creates the impression that all the excitement is happening in the present moment, increasing investor interest. Additionally, combining this effort with regular updates in a weekly newsletter will give you a strong start in your fundraising period.

## **Leverage Traction**

The third tip is to capitalise on any traction you've gained, such as securing initial term sheets. Leverage these early successes to attract higher-quality investors. If you haven't yet engaged with top-tier investors or if they've previously declined, demonstrating your momentum through traction can reignite their interest. 

## **WhatsApp**

For this next tip, you will want to make use of WhatsApp. By reaching out to investors via WhatsApp, you can prompt immediate responses, discuss terms, seek clarification, and negotiate quickly as you progress through your fundraising journey. This approach becomes especially valuable when you're fielding multiple offers, ensuring efficient communication and expediting the negotiation process.**7**

## **Build Relationships**

Invest time in building personal relationships with investors. Reach out for their advice on calls or via platforms like WhatsApp. Engaging in meaningful conversations and demonstrating your curiosity and knowledge can build rapport and increase the likelihood of securing an investment.

By consistently applying these strategies, you can effectively keep investors engaged, highlight your progress, and build the relationships necessary for a successful fundraising round.
