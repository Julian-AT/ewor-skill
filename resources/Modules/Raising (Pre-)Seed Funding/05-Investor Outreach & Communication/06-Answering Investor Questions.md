# Answering Investor Questions

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/fE-uqEI5Lp8)

This section delves into the dos and don'ts of responding to investor queries. It's not just about acing these questions; it's equally crucial to be aware of the various types of questions you might encounter.

Now, let's delve into handling difficult questions. One common mistake we see entrepreneurs make during pitches is when they are asked a question they can't answer, and instead of admitting it, they resort to dishonesty. In most cases, your potential investor will realise this, so it is counterproductive. Instead, answer truthfully. It is okay if you don’t know the answer. Let’s have a look at some responses as valuable examples that founders can use:

- **Admit You Do Not Know. **When confronted with a question to which you don’t know the answer, there are three honest and effective ways to respond. First, if you genuinely don't know the answer, admit it and express your commitment to finding out. You can say, "I'm very sorry, I don't know this, but I understand its importance. Let me look into it and come back to you tomorrow."
- **Be Transparent on Priorities. **If you don't know but believe the question is not immediately crucial, be transparent about it. You might say, "I don't know the answer to that right now, but I don't think it's a priority at this stage. X, Y, and Z are more critical, and I'm focusing on those aspects."
- **Ask for Clarification. **If you're unsure about the significance of the question, ask for clarification. You can say, "I haven't considered this before. Can you walk me through why you think it's important?" Engage in a constructive conversation to understand the context, and then express your appreciation for the insights.
