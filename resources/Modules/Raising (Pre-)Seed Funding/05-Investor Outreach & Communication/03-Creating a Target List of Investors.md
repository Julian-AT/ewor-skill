# Creating a Target List of Investors

With your CRM set up, it's time to populate it with potential investors. Building a robust list is fundamental to successful fundraising. Once you've compiled your list of potential investors, prioritise them by tagging them accordingly in your CRM. 

## 1. Start With Who You Know

Begin by adding angels, VCs, and other investors you already know or can directly reach out to. These warmest leads leverage existing relationships or mutual trust, simplifying initial conversations.

## 2. Research and Connect

Next, identify 20-30 VCs or investors who perfectly align with your fundraising goals. Use resources like TechCrunch, Google, and LinkedIn for thorough research. Beyond names, examine their past investments, focus areas, and investment thesis to identify truly relevant investors.

Determine if you have any mutual connections with this list of investors by checking their LinkedIn profiles. Cold outreach to investors is largely ineffective; VCs primarily rely on warm introductions. Studies show only about 10% of deals originate from cold inbounds, with the vast majority coming through trusted networks. If a warm connection isn't available, defer outreach. 

## 3. Leverage Your Ecosystem

Beyond direct research for investors, tap into your wider professional ecosystem. This includes other EWOR Fellows and your coach, industry event contacts and university alumni networks. Reach out to mentors, advisors, other founders, or even friends and family with strong professional ties. Ask them for investor recommendations they can connect you with. Frame requests clearly, e.g., "Who in your network might be interested in a startup focused on [your industry/problem]?" or "Do you know investors specializing in [your stage/vertical]?" This method significantly expands your list, potentially adding 20-30 high-quality leads not easily found via top-down research.

By combining these approaches – starting with your immediate network, strategically researching and seeking warm intros, and leveraging your broader ecosystem – you can build a comprehensive and effective list of investors in your CRM, setting the stage for successful outreach.
