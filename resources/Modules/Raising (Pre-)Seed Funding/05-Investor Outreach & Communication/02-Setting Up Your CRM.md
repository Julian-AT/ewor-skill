# Setting Up Your CRM

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/hoDWkFFs2W0)

Before reaching out to any investor, set up your investor CRM to keep track of your funding round. EWOR has created a CRM G-sheet template (linked below) which includes a dashboard to keep track of your (expected) fundraising amount.

## Key Columns for Your Investor CRM

We recommend that your CRM contain at least the following information:

- **Name:** The investor's full name.
- **Email Address:** Investor's primary email.
- **Phone Number:** Investor's contact number.
- **Type:** Categorisation of the investor (e.g., Angel, VC, Family Office, Other).
- **Firm:** The firm or organization the investor is associated with (if applicable).
- **Source:** How you initially discovered or connected with the investor.
- **Introduction by:** The name of the person who can introduce you or has introduced you to the investor.
- **Priority:** A ranking (e.g., 1, 2, 3, where 1 is the highest priority) to indicate investor importance.
- **Outreach Status:** Current stage of your outreach (e.g., To reach out, Reached out, Call scheduled, Call conducted, Follow-up email sent).
- **Commitment Status:** Stage of commitment from the investor (e.g., Soft Committed, Term Sheet Received, Term Sheet Signed, Investment Received, Rejected, NA).
- **Instrument:** The type of investment instrument (e.g., CLA, SAFE, ASA).
- **(Expected) Investment Amount:** The amount you expect or have received from the investor.
- **Post-Money Cap/Valuation:** The agreed-upon valuation or cap (if applicable).
- **Likelihood:** Your assessment of the probability of investment (e.g., as a percentage).
- **Notes:** Any additional relevant information or comments about the investor.

## Working with your CRM

Throughout your fundraising efforts, diligently update your CRM to prevent mistakes in reaching out to or responding to investors. 

To keep track of the expected amount you will be able to raise based on your outreach, make sure to fill in the intended investment amount per investor and their likelihood of committing. Note that as you approach more investors, the level of interest may increase exponentially rather than linearly. If you find yourself oversubscribed or gaining significant traction, use the opportunity to play the “hot startup” card. This could include raising your fundraising goal, selectively engaging with investors, and implementing other strategies to boost your startup's appeal. Remember that these tactics work best when they have a lot of traction behind them.
