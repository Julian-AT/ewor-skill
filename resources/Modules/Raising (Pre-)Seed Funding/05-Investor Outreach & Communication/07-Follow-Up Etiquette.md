# Follow-Up Etiquette

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/1nah25aToyU)

As follow-ups are critical in raising funds, this topic explores best practices in follow-up etiquette. This etiquette can be summarised in four parts:

### Speed and Professional Conduct

First and foremost, prioritise speed in your fundraising efforts. Act swiftly to capitalise on opportunities and maintain momentum.
In your communication, uphold a high level of professionalism. Do this by establishing grammatically correct emails in addition to using functional links, and provide all requested information promptly.

### Transparency

Be transparent in most aspects of your venture, including your product, customer relationships, and operations. However, be cautious when sharing specific details about your fundraising efforts. Avoid disclosing the names of other investors you're negotiating with, as this can lead to complications and power imbalances. Some investors may use this information to negotiate less favourable terms or to strengthen their negotiating position if they believe they are the sole lead. Striking a balance between openness and discretion can help you protect your negotiating position and achieve a better outcome during the fundraising process.

### Friendliness

Maintain a balance of friendliness and confidence during your interactions with investors. We recommend keeping a casual tone and avoiding appearing overly needy. It is also acceptable to let a term sheet expire; if the VC expresses interest, you can negotiate an extension. Furthermore, it is perfectly all right to communicate that you need more time to decide due to the high volume of inbound interest and discussions with other VCs. Politely inform them that if they want to secure your commitment this week, they should consider improving their offer, as other investors are offering better terms.
Be open about your reasons, stating that you have exciting discussions planned and want to make the best decision for the company. Strike a balance between being friendly and confidently asserting that your decisions are motivated by what is best for the company.

### Reminders

As a final note to follow-ups, schedule follow-up reminders and keep investors informed consistently. If a week has passed since your last communication, send a brief update summarising progress and key developments. If more information is required, politely offer it and ask if there is anything specific they want to know. Set reminders to prompt VCs who agree to provide updates but do not follow through, pushing for a definitive 'yes' or 'no'. If they mentioned getting back to you on a specific day, don't be afraid to follow up the next day with a friendly reminder, expressing your excitement and requesting the promised update. This helps maintain momentum and keeps communication on track.
