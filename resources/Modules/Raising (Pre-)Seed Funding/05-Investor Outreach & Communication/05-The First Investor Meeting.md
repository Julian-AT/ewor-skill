# The First Investor Meeting

The first call with an investor is more than just a pitch; it's an opportunity for them to get to know you as a founder. They are looking to go deeper than your deck and assess you as a person. Here’s how you can demonstrate the key qualities investors seek.

## Show Your Coachability

Investors often offer advice to see how you react. Be honest and respectful in your response. If you've considered their point before, share your thoughts. If you haven't, admit it and express your willingness to explore the idea. This shows you are open to learning and value their expertise, which is a powerful signal. While it's important to be receptive, you don't have to agree with every piece of advice. Thoughtful, constructive pushback can demonstrate an independent and discerning mindset.

## Embody Your Vision

Your ability to communicate your vision is paramount. An investor wants to know if you have a clear, emotionally resonant vision for your company. Your genuine excitement should come across naturally in your body language and tone. This sincerity is what builds a human connection and convinces investors of your long-term commitment.

## Prove Your Attractability

Investors need to be convinced you can attract top talent and customers. The most effective way to do this is through impactful stories. Share compelling anecdotes, like a customer who loved your product so much they became an employee, or a key hire who chose your company over a higher-paying offer. These stories serve as powerful, real-world evidence of your ability to persuade and build a great team.

## Convey Determination and Resilience

Investors look for unwavering determination. You must convey that your commitment is unconditional, regardless of their investment decision. Show that you are prepared to find another investor if needed, and that you have a long-term plan to sustain your business through challenges. This sense of resilience and commitment is a key indicator that you have what it takes to lead a successful company for years to come.

## Master Your Virtual Presence

Your professionalism is reflected in your Zoom setup. Ensure you have:

- **Good audio:** A quality microphone for clear sound.
- **Clean background:** A tidy, organised space.
- **Proper lighting:** Be clearly visible.
- **Strong internet:** A reliable connection to avoid interruptions.
- **Distraction-free:** Limit background noise and mute notifications.
- **Balanced camera angle:** Position your camera at eye level, filling about half the screen.

Investing in a quality ring light, camera, and microphone is a small cost compared to the potential millions you're raising. Poor audio or a distracting background can raise doubts about your professionalism and credibility.

## Be Prepared and Be Human

Practice your pitch thoroughly to project confidence and authenticity. Show genuine enthusiasm for the problem you're solving and your long-term commitment. Sincerity is vital for convincing investors of your dedication.

During the call, look directly into the camera to enhance the sense of connection. Although it may feel unnatural at first, this simple act makes you appear more engaged and focused. Where appropriate, engage in a bit of small talk to build rapport and show your human side. Remember, the impression you leave in that first virtual meeting is paramount. Attention to details like lighting, sound, and a distraction-free environment shows investors that you care and take your venture seriously.
