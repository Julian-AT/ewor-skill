# Organise Your Calendar 

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/7UOtt-IzkfQ)

Getting organised means more than staying busy — it’s about creating a system you can trust. You plan realistically, communicate clearly, and feel in control of your day. As David Allen, author of *Getting Things Done*, taught, organisation is about building a framework that frees your mind to focus on what truly matters.

For entrepreneurs, this starts with mastering your calendar. Your calendar is not a to-do list — it’s a commitment system for everything that can’t be postponed. To stay efficient, only include:

1. **Appointments** – calls, meetings, or events that happen at a fixed time.
2. **Reminders** – time-bound prompts like “follow up with Mark” or “application opens.”
3. **Deadlines** – immovable dates for submissions or launches.

Everything else belongs on your to-do list or project tracker. Mixing flexible tasks with fixed events makes your calendar unreliable, leading to missed meetings and poor prioritisation. The goal is to make your calendar a single source of truth for time-bound commitments, a tool you respect completely.

### Adopt a Make and Manage Schedule

When organising your week, a make and manage schedule helps balance focus and coordination.

- **Make (Morning):** Block time for deep, creative, or strategic work.
- **Manage (Afternoon):** Reserve this period for meetings, admin tasks, and communication.

This rhythm balances focus with structure. It helps you use your most productive hours for creation and your later hours for coordination. To keep your calendar lean and effective:

- **Stack similar tasks.** Group calls or meetings back-to-back to avoid context switching.
- **Limit calls.** Many discussions are better handled asynchronously. Before scheduling, ask: Does this call have a clear goal or agenda? Will it lead to a better decision or faster execution? Is it needed to enforce a deadline or accountability?
If not, use a message, audio note, or written update instead.
- **Separate thinking and doing.** Use your to-do list for flexible tasks and ideas. Keep your calendar sacred for fixed time slots.

Once your calendar is structured, make sure your tasks are actionable. Every item you capture should translate into a concrete next step, not a vague aspiration. Replace “Integrate CRM system” with “Schedule developer sync on CRM integration.” This approach bridges your to-do list and your calendar, turning plans into action and keeping momentum steady without overwhelm.
