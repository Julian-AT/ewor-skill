# Introduction to Time Management

How you spend your time determines everything—your success, fulfilment, and impact. Once your goals are clear, managing time becomes your most powerful tool for achieving them.

The challenge lies in choice. You can have anything, but not everything. Trying to do it all leads to distraction and burnout; focusing on what truly matters creates progress. Time management, at its core, is the art of saying “no.”

Think of it like moving through fog. When you’re deep in daily tasks, it’s hard to see the big picture. Stepping back to reflect and plan is like climbing a hill—you regain perspective and direction. But planning alone isn’t enough; you must return to action and move forward.

![Effectiveness and efficiency are the forces that drive effective time management.](/api/media/file/PD-L3T1-IntroToTimeManagement-1.png)

Effective time management balances two forces:

- **Effectiveness:** choosing the right goals and direction.
- **Efficiency:** executing quickly and wisely.

As Greg McKeown said, “If you don’t prioritise your life, someone else will.” Most people react to what happens around them; entrepreneurs must design their own game. Stay proactive, keep your priorities clear, and focus on what truly moves you toward your vision.

At EWOR, we turn time management into practice through daily and weekly checklists. These simple tools bring structure and consistency to your day:

- **Daily checklist:** This list keeps you proactive. It should include key habits like communication, planning, and small behaviour improvements. Start small, two to three items are enough. Then over time, add one each month as the routine sticks.
- **Weekly checklist:** This list helps you reflect and reset. Review how you spent your time, identify what created the most value, plan the week ahead, and clear your backlog.

Together, these checklists create the foundation of effective time management. They make you more intentional with each hour and ensure your effort stays aligned with what truly moves you forward.

## Diving Deeper

- **Deep Work by Cal Newport**
In a time when many people seem to have lost the ability to focus on cognitively demanding tasks due to notifications constantly tugging at our attention, Cal Newport explains why Deep Work will set you ahead of the competition and how to do it.
- **Getting Things Done by David Allen**
David Allen developed the personal productivity system called Getting Things Done. The bestseller, sometimes even described as the bible for personal productivity, lays out a clear system that is easy to follow.
- **The Checklist Manifesto by Atul Gawande**
Even in routine settings, highly skilled people make avoidable mistakes. In The Checklist Manifesto, Dr. Gawande makes a case for a simple solution: checklists.
