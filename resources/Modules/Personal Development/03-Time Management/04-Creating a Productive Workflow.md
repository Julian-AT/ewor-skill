# Creating a Productive Workflow

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/LySRYwRodY4)

A great productivity system helps you manage communication, tasks, and focus with clarity. The goal isn’t to do more—it’s to make every action deliberate, aligned, and efficient. Below are the key elements of a system that keeps you in control of your work and progress.

## 1. Build a Unified Inbox

Most people lose track of tasks because their communication is fragmented across too many platforms. Consolidate everything—email, Slack, WhatsApp—into one unified inbox (or two at most).

Forward all your email accounts into one inbox, such as Gmail or Superhuman, and configure replies to appear from the correct address. This keeps your communication organised and makes Inbox Zero achievable. You don’t have to respond to everything immediately, but you should *see everything* and make conscious decisions about what to address, delay, or delete.

## 2. Make Every Task Actionable

Once you’ve captured all tasks and ideas, refine them into actionable items—the smallest, concrete next step you can take. Avoid vague entries like “Build CRM system.” Instead, write “Schedule developer sync on CRM integration.” This makes execution frictionless.

## 3. Apply the Focus Funnel

With actionable tasks in place, use David Allen’s** Focus Funnel** to process your to-do list efficiently:

1. **Eliminate:** Ask, *Can I remove this?* Many ideas are “nice to have” but not relevant to your OKRs or stretch goals. Delete them.
2. **Automate:** If the task can be handled by a tool or system, automate it. Building systems first—like automatic accounting or email follow-ups—dramatically boosts long-term productivity.
3. **Delegate:** Ask, *Who has a comparative advantage?* Delegation isn’t limited to hierarchy—you can delegate upward if it makes sense. The best person for a task is the one who can do it faster or better without neglecting higher-priority work.
4. **Procrastinate (Productively):** Not everything needs to be done now. Move non-urgent tasks to a “Not Yet” list or assign them to a future project.
5. **Do It (Two-Minute Rule):** If it takes less than two minutes, complete it immediately.
6. **Leave It On Your List:** Prio the remaining tasks by importance, then block time for it tomorrow. If your schedule is full, re-evaluate: is what’s currently in your calendar more important than your top priority?
