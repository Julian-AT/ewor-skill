# Track Your Time

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/-tHpG-1SFP4)

For entrepreneurs, every second is a chance you can’t get back. Every day wasted is 5% of your month; every hour wasted is 12.5% of your workday. You can always add more hours, but you can never recover lost ones. Mindful time management begins with tracking where your time actually goes. Tracking your time helps you see the gap between what you *plan* to do and what you *actually* do. 

The science backs this up. Studies show that individuals who track their time:

- Become more aware of task-switching and interruptions, improving focus and efficiency.
- Reduce stress by understanding how disruptions affect their work.
Gain insights into job patterns, increasing satisfaction and well-being.

In one experiment by Czerwinski, Horvitz, and Wilhite (2004), it was shown that professionals who tracked their time became significantly more productive than those who didn’t. They wasted less effort on low-value tasks and made better decisions about where to focus. Simply logging time made them more self-aware—and more effective.

At EWOR, we translate this insight into action through daily time tracking and weekly reviews. It takes about seven minutes a day but can return hours of regained productivity. 

> The goal to spend roughly 80% of your time on your key goals and 20% on everything else.

Start by tracking your time for one week. Review it honestly, identify where you’re off-course, and make adjustments. This simple habit is one of the fastest ways to reclaim focus, align your effort with your priorities, and make every hour count.

### Using a Time Tracker

When tracking your time, start by logging your time daily using a simple tracking template, such as [The EWOR Life Tracker](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1wKH6FKBkwBUBkBFCgz1r9kpjGCLgA9fGCqsq-izTvrQ/template/preview) or one of the apps listed below. For one full week, log your time in 30-minute blocks. Categorise each activity based on how close it is to your main goals:

- **Top 5 priorities**. Tasks directly connected to your weekly goals, which stem from your quarterly OKRs and long-term stretch vision.
- **Important surprises.** Unplanned but necessary tasks that arise during the week, such as urgent team issues or unexpected opportunities.
- **Flexible time. **Activities of your choice—creative exploration, rest, or low-stakes work that keeps you balanced.

At the end of each week, review your log to see where your time went. How much of it truly advanced your top priorities? If you’re off balance the 80:20 balance, adjust the following week.

You can also track your total hours of meaningful work to spot patterns—when you’re most productive, what drains focus, and what consistently drives progress. Over time, this habit builds awareness, focus, and control over how you invest your most limited resource.
