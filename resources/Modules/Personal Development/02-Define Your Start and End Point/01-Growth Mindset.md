# Growth Mindset

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/v9Fe06gjODs)

A growth mindset is the belief that your abilities, skills, and intelligence can be developed over time through effort, feedback, and learning. This mindset isn’t just motivational—it’s foundational for any founder who wants to grow faster than their challenges.

## What Is a Growth Mindset?

Psychologist Carol Dweck popularised the concept in her book Mindset. People with a fixed mindset believe traits like intelligence or talent are static—you’re either born with it or not. Those with a growth mindset believe they can improve through learning, reflection, and persistence.

The difference becomes obvious in how people react to challenges:

- **Fixed mindset: **Avoids difficulties, fears failure, blames external factors.
- **Growth mindset:** Sees failure as feedback, takes ownership, and actively looks for ways to improve.

### Why It Matters for Founders

As a founder, you’ll face countless setbacks: investor rejections, hiring challenges, and failed experiments. If you believe these are signs you’re “not cut out for this,” you’ll likely give up. But with a growth mindset, you treat each challenge as a learning opportunity.

You don’t have to get it right on the first try—you just have to get better with each try.

> The growth mindset is an exponential function. Small, consistent efforts now compound into long-term advantages.

## Mindsets Are Not Fixed

You might have a growth mindset in one area (e.g., leadership) but a fixed mindset in another (e.g., maths, fundraising). That’s normal. Your mindset can shift based on the situation—and it can be trained over time.

However, developing a growth mindset is only the first step—translating it into consistent action is where transformation happens. The bridge between belief and outcome lies in the systems you build. Productivity systems turn mindset into motion: they help you align your focus, energy, and time with your growth goals.
