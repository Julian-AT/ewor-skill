# Determine Your Starting Point

As mentioned earlier, it’s important to get a clear snapshot of where you are right now. This will serve as your starting point. To help with that, we recommend completing the questionnaires and self-assessments below.

**Enneagram**

The Enneagram is a personality framework that identifies nine core types, each reflecting distinct motivations, fears, and patterns of behaviour. By taking [this Enneagram test](https://personalitypath.com/test/enneagram/en), you can gain deeper self-awareness—understanding what drives your decisions, how you relate to others, and where your growth edges lie. While not scientifically definitive, it’s a valuable tool for personal reflection and emotional intelligence development. For an in depth review of your type, we recommend this [Enneagram type overview](https://www.enneagraminstitute.com/type-descriptions/). 

**MBTI**

Go to [this site](https://www.16personalities.com/free-personality-test) and fill in the questionnaire to determine our MBTI profile. MBTI, or Myers-Briggs Type Indicator, was developed by Katharine Cook Briggs and her daughter Isabel Briggs Myers and categorises people along 4 axes. Although there is limited scientific evidence supporting the test’s validity—and it’s even been jokingly called “astrology for coworkers”—we believe its results can still offer valuable insights into your personality and preferences.

**Strengthsfinder**

Another test we highly recommend is the [Gallup Strengthsfinder test](https://www.gallup.com/cliftonstrengths/en/252137/home.aspx). The test was developed by Don Clifton and therefore sometimes dubbed the Clifton Strengths Test. It is a personality assessment tool that breaks personalities down into 34 themes. It is a great tool to understand your personality better and find areas in which you want to improve.
