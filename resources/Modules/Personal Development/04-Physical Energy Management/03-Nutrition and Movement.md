# Nutrition and Movement

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/AKzsYZ9jIZo)

Your body is the foundation of your performance. No system, mindset, or strategy can replace the physical energy needed to sustain long hours of focus, creativity, and decision-making. Physical energy comes down to three essential levers: what you put in, how you move, and how you recover.

## Energy In: Nutrition and Awareness

What you eat directly determines how long you can sustain your output. Founders often treat nutrition as an afterthought, living on caffeine and convenience food, but your fuel defines your capacity. Start with awareness: what are you consuming each day, and how does it affect your long-term performance?

The potential benefits of improved focus, memory, and learning can be invaluable in the fast-paced world of entrepreneurship. However, nutrition and fasting should both be approached mindfully. Not every fasting method suits everyone, and individual responses can vary. If you experiment with fasting, start slowly, listen to your body, and consider professional guidance. Balance it with nutrient-rich meals—especially those that support brain health and concentration. Essential fatty acids, antioxidants, and key vitamins and minerals are all critical for cognitive performance.

### What to Eat for Maximum Productivity

- **Omega-3 fats and magnesium.** Found in nuts, avocados, and fish; both support higher BDNF release, better brain health, and long-term cognitive function.
- **Protein.** Sources include beans, eggs, fish, nuts, and lean meats. A protein-rich breakfast sustains focus and energy throughout the day.
- **Vegetables.** Provide essential antioxidants and micronutrients that protect cells and support overall vitality.
- **Complex carbohydrates.** Choose brown rice, oats, and whole grains for steady, lasting energy instead of sugar crashes.
- **Water and fresh juice.** Maintain hydration and support brain function; natural fructose from fruit behaves differently than refined sugar.
- **Natural stimulants.** Coffee, black tea, matcha, or guarana enhance alertness when consumed earlier in the day.

### What to Avoid

- **Trans and saturated fats.** Found in processed meats and oils, they impair blood flow, increase inflammation, and reduce mental clarity and cardiovascular health.
- **Sugary drinks, sweets, and refined carbs.** Cause blood sugar spikes followed by energy crashes, leading to fatigue and poor concentration.
- **Alcohol.** Disrupts neurotransmitters, worsens REM sleep, and dehydrates the body, lowering next-day focus, energy, and mood.

## Energy Out: Movement and Maintenance

Exercise isn’t optional—it’s how you preserve your long-term performance capacity. Long hours of sitting, travelling, and screen time strain your body and dull your mind. Movement restores balance, sharpens focus, and builds resilience. Physical activity not only strengthens your body but also regulates dopamine, improving motivation, mood, and concentration while reducing stress and burnout risk.

You don’t have to love fitness to benefit from it—treat it as preventive maintenance. Just as you manage your company to sustain growth, you manage your body to sustain performance. Combine **resistance training** to build strength and muscle with **light cardio** to enhance endurance, brain oxygenation, and recovery.

### The Goal: Balance and Consistency

Aim for **body recomposition**—increasing muscle while reducing fat. This balance stabilises hormones, supports better sleep, and boosts energy and confidence. Even simple routines performed consistently can rewire your neurochemistry for sharper focus, improved emotional control, and long-term productivity.
