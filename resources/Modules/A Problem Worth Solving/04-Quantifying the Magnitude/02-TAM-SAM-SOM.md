# TAM-SAM-SOM

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/matDgj0ZH4s)

TAM-SAM-SOM are three often used metrics related to market size or opportunity. It is a tiered approach, moving from the broadest possible market definition to a portion of the opportunity you can realistically capture in the next few years.

### **TAM – Total Available Market**

The total market demand for a product or service if everyone who needs it were to purchase it. This represents the overall potential market size without any limitations.

### **SAM – Serviceable Available Market**

The segment of the TAM that can realistically be reached and served with your future resources and capabilities. This takes into account factors like geographic reach, target customer segments, distribution channels and your capabilities in resources 5 to 10 years from now.

### **SOM – Serviceable Obtainable Market**

The portion of SAM that can realistically be captured in the short term, considering your marketing budget, sales team, competition, and other factors. This represents your most immediate revenue potential.

![TAM-SAM-SOM is often visualised as three overlapping circles.](/api/media/file/TAM-SAM-SOM.png)

Note that to be able to calculate SAM and SOM, you would need a good understanding of the solution you will offer as well as your business module. As this phase, the focus is on finding a problem worth solving rather than developing a solution. Once you reach viability,  calculating SAM and SOM will become more relevant.

Nevertheless, thinking about SAM and SOM and visualising them, with the note that those are first assumptions, shows early possible investors that you have a feeling for your market and its size.
