# Identifying Current Solutions

Finding the companies that are your direct and indirect competitors is not always easy. Especially because some of them are likely startups that haven’t necessarily received lots of media attention (yet). They might still be in quite an early stage and not turn up on your Google Search.

To make sure you identify the majority of your competitors, we recommend the following tools:

- **Interviewees From Your Need-Finding Research. **If you asked your interviewees how they currently solved the problem, it is likely that they mentioned the name of a company. Since this company is solving the same problem for the same customer, they are a competitor!
- **Leverage LinkedIn. **LinkedIn has a “Similar Pages” section on every business page. This allows you to see the other company pages that visitors of a competitor's page have visited. Chances are that some of those will be solving the problem you are researching.
- **Become a Customer. **Put yourself in the shoes of your target customers, and try to solve the problem yourself. What solution (and companies) do you arrive at after doing your research and narrowing down your options to the (current) best picks?
- **Google. **Search for companies that solve the problem you are researching using google. Try different keywords and see what you find.
- **Industry Reports. **Search for industry reports. Some VCs or research agencies make really good industry maps which include startups. [HolonIQ](https://www.holoniq.com/) for instance does this for climate, education, and healthcare.
- **Leverage Your (EWOR) Network. **Identifying startups that solve the problem you are researching is no easy task. The EWOR Network, however, collectively, has interacted with a huge number of startups! Ask in a general Slack channel if anyone knows of a startup solving the problem. In addition, make sure to ask your coach if they know of any startups. Another source could be the EWOR Mentors. Search for a mentor with experience in your industry and request an introduction to them to ask them if they know of any startups in this space.

When researching solutions, make sure to capture the information you find. We recommend using Excel or Google Sheets to keep track of the competitors you find. We have created a [template](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1P1jBKxWwDeujYU0gpdAGoLbYNuBySt4U2najdDwYzB0/edit?usp=sharing) for you that you can use, but feel free to create your own! Note that not all columns are relevant for every competitor. On LinkedIn, you might be able to find the number of employees. Crunchbase, Dealroom.com or TechCrunch are websites on which you might be able to find out whether a startup has received funding and who the investors are.
