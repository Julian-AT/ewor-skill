# Visualising the Current Solutions

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/aGjCyoZkO8U)

Once you've gathered information on existing solutions, it can be beneficial to visualise the competitive landscape. This will help you identify opportunities for differentiation and innovation. Two often-used visualisations are the Competition Matrix and the Positioning Map.

## Competition Matrix

A Competition Matrix visualises competitors side-by-side, making it easier to spot opportunities. List out the key features, pricing, target audience, or anything important about the product or service in the rows.  in the columns, the main competitors are listed. Fill the matrix in using checkmarks, short descriptions, or ratings to see how each competitor stacks up. Look for gaps where competitors are falling short or where a customer need isn't being met. This can offer inspiration for your solution. Remember Clayton Christensen's Disruptive Innovation Theory: could a simpler, cheaper product actually be better and disrupt the market?

## Positioning Map

A positioning map helps visualise the competitive landscape by plotting companies on a grid based on two defining attributes. These attributes should reflect what customers value and highlight how competitors differ.

![A positioning map](/api/media/file/M2-L4T5-PositioningMap.png)

Below, you can see an example of a positioning map. We mapped social media and communication platforms based on two attributes: intimacy and whether the platform is mostly focused on sharing text or images.
