# Different Types of Competitors 

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/S41YPPMEmzM)

In competitive analysis, different types of competitors are distinguished based on how they differ from the benchmark company. As you likely have not developed a solution yet, this benchmark is currently lacking. However, we do believe that knowledge of the different types of solutions available can help you get a better understanding of available solutions.

## Direct Competitors

Direct competitors offer the same or very similar products or services to the same target market, addressing the same core customer need. They are the most critical competitors because the competition is often a zero-sum game—if customers choose your competitor, they likely won’t choose you. Direct competitors compete directly on price, features, quality, and brand. Think of classic rivalries like Coca-Cola vs. Pepsi, Nike vs. Adidas, or McDonald's vs. Burger King. In the tech world, Google Search and Bing Search are direct competitors.

## Indirect Competitors

Indirect competitors offer different products or services that solve the same fundamental customer need or problem for the same target market. Their offerings are substitutes for each other. A movie theater and Netflix both provide entertainment, but through different channels. Similarly, a bicycle company and a public transportation system both offer transportation. ChatGPT and Google Search are indirect competitors; both provide information, but in vastly different ways.

## Potential Competitors

Potential competitors are companies that are not currently competing with you (directly or indirectly) but could do so in the future. In this broad category, there are two we want to highlight:

- Geographic Expansion: Just Eat Takeaway (dominant in European meal delivery) and Swiggy/Zomato (leaders in India) currently serve different markets. However, either could potentially expand geographically, becoming direct competitors. Even in cases where geographic expansion is unlikely, studying competitors targeting a different market can inspire ideas for your solution.
- Market Leaders with Resources: Established companies like Google and Meta, with their vast resources and user bases, are constantly exploring new markets. Their entry into AI chatbots and agents demonstrates how quickly they can become competitors in emerging fields. If you're developing an AI chatbot, these tech giants are potential competitors you need to consider.
