# Reflections

Before moving on to the next module, we highly recommend that you reflect on the questions and statements below and discuss them with your coach.

### Can conclusions be drawn based on my research?

-  I have interviewed at least 30 people
-  I have observed at least 5 people
-  I have done at least 1 self-immersion
-  I have interviewed and observed people from a wide range of demographic backgrounds
-  I have gathered lots of concrete statements about the past rather than non-committal statements about the future

### Is the problem worth solving?

**Is it a true problem?**

-  I have interviewed and observed a fair amount of people who have the problem, were aware they have the problem, and are actively searching for solutions (early adoptors)
-  Actual behaviour aligns with detected behaviour for most research subject

**Is the problem of high magnitude?**

-  People who experience the problem feel it deeply, meaning the problem costs them a lot of time, money and/or emotional pain
-  Many people experience the problem, resulting in a significant market size (or market opportunity)

**Is the problem unavoidable?**

-  The hacks that people experiencing the problem came up with do not solve the problem to their satisfaction. The hack might take more time, cost extra money, result in dangerous situations, or affect someone’s dignity.
-  Existing solutions from incumbents do not solve the problem to the satisfaction of many people experiencing the problem. Either the solution is too expensive or overserves the market.

### Is my Point of View clear and concrete?

-  The PoV reflects the data gathered through need-finding
-  The PoV focusses on user value but remains solution-agnostic
-  The PoV is inspiring and guides innovation while informing evaluation of potential solutions
-  The PoV addresses a context need or an activity need

### Do I have Founder-Problem Fit?

-  I have a strong desire to solve the problem and am convinced I am passionate enough about the problem to dedicate years of my life to the endeavor
-  I have relevant experience in the industry or domain related to the problem
-  I have connections within the target market that can provide insights and support and can turn into my first hires or customers

If you have answered ‘no’ to any of the above questions or statements about the research you did or the problem, we highly recommend spending some more time validating your problem or improving your problem statement. In case you answered ‘no’ to any of the statements regarding founder-problem fit, you might want to reconsider whether this is the problem you want to solve.
