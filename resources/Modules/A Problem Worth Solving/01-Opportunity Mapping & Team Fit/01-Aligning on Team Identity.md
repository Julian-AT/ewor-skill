# Aligning on Team Identity

Before you can explore markets or brainstorm opportunities, you need to understand where you and your team stand. The first question isn’t “What’s out there?” but “What’s right for us?” Getting this alignment right early on prevents wasted effort and sets the foundation for everything that follows.

This reflection has two parts, which you can work on in parallel:

1. The right idea for you and your team
2. The right idea for the market (which means the players in it)

The starting point, however, is always you** **and your team.

## Find Your Ikigai

A helpful framework here is the Japanese concept of Ikigai, which combines what you love, what you’re good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for. Start by drawing the four overlapping circles of the Ikigai chart. In each circle, write concrete examples in…

- **What you love**. List hobbies or topics that energise you.
- **What you’re good at. **Capture both hard skills (like coding, design, sales) and soft skills (like empathy or leadership).
- **What the world needs. **Think about problems you see around you or causes you care about.
- **What you can be paid for.** Consider industries, roles, or business models where people already spend money. The intersections of these circles are where motivation and opportunity begin to align.

Each team member should complete their Ikigai individually first. When you’re able to connect passion to profession and see how that links to broader needs, you start to uncover not just what excites you, but what can sustain you over time.

## Tying in the Team Canvas

Once your team has defined their personal Ikigai, bring them together using a Team Canvas. This is where individual insights turn into a shared foundation. The Team Canvas helps you align on how you want to work together, what excites you collectively, where your strengths lie, and which areas you want to avoid. These “red lines” are as important as your passions. Some teams, for example, choose from the outset to exclude military applications or regulated substances because they conflict with their values. By agreeing on boundaries early, you avoid painful debates later and focus on the opportunity spaces that truly fit your team.

![Achieve productive, stress-free teamwork by aligning in key areas with this template by Team Canvas.](/api/media/file/M1-TheTeamCanvas.png)

The result of this exercise is not a final idea, but a first draft of your team identity: how you want to collaborate, what drives you, and where you won’t go. This clarity becomes your compass and ensures that when you start exploring opportunities, you’re looking in the right places.
