# Visualising Your Data

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/nihSXqKsOwA)

Synthesis begins with visualising your data—a technique called “space saturation” in Design Thinking. Start by gathering all your information and writing each data point on a separate sticky note. Whenever possible, use direct quotes and clearly attribute them. Include any relevant photos, drawings, or other artefacts collected during your need-finding research.

If you’re working in a team, have each member prepare their sticky notes in advance. Then, take turns presenting them on a whiteboard, explaining the context and insights behind each one. This collaborative approach ensures that everyone develops a shared understanding of the data and sets the foundation for meaningful synthesis.
