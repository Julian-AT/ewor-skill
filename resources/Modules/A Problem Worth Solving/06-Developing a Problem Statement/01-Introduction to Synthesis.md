# Introduction to Synthesis

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/H3booB3U8ug)

By conducting need-finding, market sizing, and competitor analysis, you’ve determined whether the problem you’re researching is worth solving. Now, it’s time to synthesise the data you’ve gathered and formulate a problem statement, which will provide a clear focus for ideation—the next module we recommend.

Synthesis is the process of combining ideas and information to create something new. It’s about crafting a coherent story that explains how the different components fit together. Unlike analysis, which involves breaking things apart and examining individual pieces, synthesis is about connecting the dots and seeing the bigger picture.

This process is both creative and subjective, meaning different people working with the same data might arrive at different stories and problem statements. There’s no definitive right or wrong in synthesis. However, it’s crucial to be thorough and spend plenty of time on it. This allows your brain to fully process the data and uncover fresh insights, leading to a well-rounded problem statement that sets the stage for ideation.

To synthesise your data, we’ll discuss three steps:

- Visualising your data
- Clustering your data
- Developing a problem statement

As synthesis is an iterative and creative process, we highly recommend doing this in the physical world rather than on your computer. Especially when you work in a team, standing together in front of a whiteboard and being able to manipulate the data sparks creativity. You are more immersed in the stories. There is something about being able to move around the space. Take a literal step back or look at it from a different angle that helps you see connections that you might have missed in a limited screen version of the same board. In addition, using a marker is often quicker and more flexible than digital tools, allowing you to better express thoughts and ideas.
