# Exploring Different Business Models

Now that you’ve understood how relevant business models are, it is important that you develop a feeling for the variety of different business models that are available to you. We highly recommend the [Business Model Navigator](https://businessmodelnavigator.com/explore) by Oliver Gassman from the University of St. Gallen for you to develop such a feeling for business models. Take ten minutes and click on the link above and read through all business models.

If you want, you can further dig into the companies utilising these business models and dig deeper into how exactly those models unfold when being used by startups or incumbent corporations. Often, the business model itself, not the product, is the key to the success of the business. 

We furthermore recommend that you check out the following video that also goes into explaining why specific business models work better than others. 

[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DL6myRFdC_g)

Dollar Shave Club is probably one of the most impressive examples of how a new business model disrupted an entire industry. Dollar Shave Club built a subscription model for razors, which was soon acquired by Unilever for 1 BN dollars. We recommend you listen to [this podcast](https://podcasts.apple.com/bs/podcast/live-episode-dollar-shave-club-michael-dubin/id1150510297?i=1000425929182) and read this [case study](https://www.feedough.com/dollar-shave-club-business-model-case-study/) to get more familiar with the company. It’s a good example that helps you grasp the relevance of business modelling. 

## How to Conceptualise Your Business Model

When conceptualising your business model, you can use a variety of tools, such as

- [The Business Model Canvas](https://www.strategyzer.com/canvas/business-model-canvas)
- [The Value Proposition Canvas](https://www.b2binternational.com/research/methods/faq/what-is-the-value-proposition-canvas/)
- [The Product Canvas](https://miro.com/templates/product-canvas/) (for specific products)

We recommend that you create a Word document or PowerPoint/G Slides presentation for each of the three and try it out yourself.
