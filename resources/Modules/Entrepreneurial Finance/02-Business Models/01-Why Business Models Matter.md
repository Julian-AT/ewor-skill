# Why Business Models Matter

On the simplest level, a business model is a description of how an organisation: 

- **Creates** with customers 
- **Delivers **through its technology and systems
- **Captures** through its revenue and cost model value.

One can say that a business model is the companies’ plan on how to generate revenues. It includes answers to three core questions:

- Who is the customer?
- What does the customer value?
- How do we make money in this business?

Your business model is crucial for building a successful business as it structures your operations, gives your employees a direction, and allows you to set goals for managers. A business without a clearly defined business model is likely going to fail. But a business model isn’t purely about financials and revenue calculations, a good business model is all about telling a compelling story. If it has a flaw, it is likely one of the following:

1. The story is not good enough (it doesn’t make sense).
2. The numbers don’t add up (the P&L doesn’t add up).

In some way, a business model is the equivalent to a scientific hypothesis: You create it based on the information you have, test it in practice, and adjust it, if necessary, to create a valid theory in the end.
