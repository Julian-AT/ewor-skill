# Introduction to Financial Statements

In order to make an investment decision, investors want to evaluate their potential investment in the company by analysing its financial statements. [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/f/financial-statements.asp) defines financial statements as follows:

> Financial statements are written records that convey the business activities and the financial performance of a company. They are often audited by government agencies, accountants, firms, etc. to ensure accuracy and for tax, financing, or investing purposes.

In this lesson, we will discuss the three core records of financial statements that every business needs to have readily available:

- **Balance sheet**:** **Illustrates a company’s *assets *(what they own), its *liabilities *(what they owe), as well as its *equity* (what the owner and shareholders have invested) 
- **Statement of Cash Flows **(Cash Flow Statement)**: **Shows the cash inflow and outflow as well as the overall net change of cash of the company over a specific period of time
- **Profit & Loss Statement **(Income Statement):** **Is a summary of all revenues and expenses of a company over a given period of time
