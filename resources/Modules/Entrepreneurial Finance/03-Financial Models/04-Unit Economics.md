# Unit Economics

This section is not mandatory for every financial model. However, every good software business or business based on online distribution, should have a proper unit economics calculation. A perfectly calculated unit economics calculation will provide you with an answer of whether your business will stay fundamentally profitable. Moreover, calculating profit per unit is the best possible way for:

1. Understanding which aspects you need to twist to become more profitable.
2. Understanding the upper ceiling of your EBITDA margin (as these are costs you will never be able to reduce, even though you’d have billions of users).
3. Developing a gut feeling based on relevant ratios (e.g. LTV/CAC) of how likely you will be to succeed.
4. Developing an understanding of how valuable each newly won customer is, independent of the aggregate yearly turnover you are making.

After you have completed your unit economics calculation, check for the following:

- Is your LTV/CAC greater than 3:1?
- Will your LTV/CAC ratio grow over time? If so, which credible arguments do you have for backing this up?
