# Growth Assumptions

## Making Reasonable Assumptions About Your Growth

In your revenue forecast, you want to make sure to make your sales or user growth more tangible. You want to create at least a yearly forecast about your growth, but, in the best case, have a monthly estimation ready to better estimate your growth within the first year. Make sure that your growth assumptions are compatible with your costs.

For example, if your business model requires every user to be onboarded for at least half an hour. You would assume that your customer support and/or onboarding team grows with your user growth. You should have a quick calculation ready, such as: One onboarding team member can onboard 100 users in one month. This makes 1,200 per year and justifies that with 10,000 new users, roughly 9 new onboarding staff members need to be hired.
