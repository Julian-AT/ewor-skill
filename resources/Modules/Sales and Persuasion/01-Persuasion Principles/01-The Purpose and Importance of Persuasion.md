# The Purpose and Importance of Persuasion

Dr. Robert Cialdini, a leading influence researcher, defines persuasion as a communication process for influencing other people to move, motivate, or enact a change in attitude or behavior by using spoken or written words. (Cialdini, 1984)

Conversely, he also destroys common-held connotations of persuasion, stating that the process is NOT a method of manipulation, coercion, or any other method of getting someone to do something that is not in their best interest. (Cialdini, 1984) The most useful view you can hold about persuasion is that it’s a necessary, beneficial communication process that will help bring your project to life, and it occurs in virtually all human to human interactions.

Why is persuasion relevant to you? Well as your project gains traction and scales, you’re going to be responsible for persuading prospects to buy your solution, persuading people to join your team, and persuading investors to deploy capital into your business, among many other use cases. Persuasion is a core skill set in all successful entrepreneurs, and the success of your project hinges on your ability to be persuasive and influence relevant stakeholders.
