# Communicating Based On Customer Persona

Successful sales communication hinges on an understanding of who your prospects are and how you can influence them. We’ll start by looking at the minimum standards for a B2B customer persona, and then switch gears to B2C. (Even though all businesses are P2P.) Regardless of what your solution is, you need to understand both your prospects’ demographics (who your prospect is) and psychographics (why they buy).

For B2B demographics, you have to consider the Targeted Role, Industry, and Company Size so you can identify who your prospects are. An example of these demographic factors is all Product Marketers who work in cybersecurity at a company with at least 250 employees. 

**Pro-tip** – the larger the organization, the stricter the standard operating protocols and the more difficult/standardized the procurement procedure is.* 

To grasp why these B2B prospects buy, consider the possible wants, needs, fears, desires, and obstacles your prospect faces. The Product Marketer wants to keep their job and needs to be validated by their office peers. Even though they desire a promotion, they also fear making a mistake that will lead to them being embarrassed or fired. This means self-doubt is the Product Marketer’s biggest obstacle since it plagues their ability to make a decision. 

Psychographic factors are the same for B2C prospects – all that changes are the demographic factors. You need to consider your prospects’ age, gender, socioeconomic parameters, and other relevant information that can identify who your prospect is. For example, maybe your prospect is a 42-year-old man who averages $250K per year in salary and works in the financial services industry. 

On top of this information, you’ll also want to identify any jargon that your target persona uses, so you can mirror it back to them and engage the liking principle from the last lesson.

Most, if not all, of the information needed for your customer persona can be gathered from your primary market research, as well as interviews with successful beta customers.

These factors matter to you because you’ll be able to identify where you can find more ideal customers in the market AND tailor your offer to give a prospect what they want without exposing them to the risk of experiencing what they fear.
