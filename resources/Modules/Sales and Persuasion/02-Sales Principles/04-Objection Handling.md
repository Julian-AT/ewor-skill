# Objection Handling

“No.”

While most salespeople start running when they hear no, this is actually where the sale really begins.

As we discussed earlier, objections come from a state where your prospect is feeling fear and resistance. The fear and resistance are keeping them stuck in their current state, and if anything, you’re morally obligated to help someone out of their painful state. Belfort likens handling objections to picking a lock; once all the tumblers are down, you’re ready to open the safe and make a sale.

An effective way to pick through objections is with active listening and asking why in a compassionate manner. Your prospect will give you clues about what’s holding them back. For example, ever hear the famous objection “I need to speak with my partner/spouse?” On the surface, it sounds like your prospect needs to consult with another person, but what they’re most likely saying between the lines is that there is too much risk to move forward. A sales pro acknowledges that they need to speak with their partner/spouse, introduce a risk reversal for the prospect (money-back guarantee, etc), summarize what’s in it for them again, and then ask for the sale again.
