# An Overview of Pricing Your Solution

We’re onto the final lesson, how to price your solution! Pricing is an imperfect science, since some experts argue for cost-based and others argue value-based or other methods. What I’ll be sharing with you is a quick decision tree you can follow to determine how to best price your solution based on your unique solution. For a more theoretical, business-model lens, you can alternatively check out our [lesson on pricing](https://education.ewor.io/lessons/revenue-model/).

## Building a B2B Solution

If you’re building a B2B solution, here is your decision tree.

1. What is the specific problem you’re solving?
2. Can you quantify the annual dollar value of the problem you solve? For example:
3. 1. Amount of additional revenue created per year attributable to your solution AND/OR
2. Amount of employee working hours saved per year AND/OR
3. Amount of other overhead/expenditures saved in a year AND/OR
4. Amount of risk exposure you’ve mitigated AND/OR
5. Amount of operational efficiencies created AND/OR
6. Amount of churn reduced AND/OR
7. Impact of improved quality on customers' retention/operations
8. Etc
4. If you can quantify the problem value, are your unit economics profitable if you charge 1/10th as your solutions price? (Profitable = ~30% net margins)
5. If you could not answer question #2 OR you are not profitable at question #3, determine your customers’ willingness to pay (WTP) by asking them.
6. If you’re not profitable at their WTP, then price your solution to generate at least ~30% net margins.

## Building a B2C Solution

If you’re building a B2C solution, here is your decision tree. 

1. What is your customer persona(e) WTP for the problem you solve? For example:
2. 1. Time saved
2. Ease created
3. Getting a customer(e) to their desired identity
4. Giving access to a community/feeling connected
5. Exclusivity
6. Quality
7. Other ways your solution solves their problem/adds value
3. Are you able to make at least a 30% net margin based on this WTP?
4. If not, price at whatever generates a 30% net margin based on your unit economics
