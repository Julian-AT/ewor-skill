### Selling Shoes Without Inventory

In 1999, Nick Swinmurn had the idea of selling shoes online. His leap-of-faith assumption was that enough people would buy shoes online if there were no delivery or return fees. To test this assumption, he went to a local shoe store, took pictures of their shoes and posted them on his website. 

When someone bought a pair of shoes on the website, he would go to the store to buy them and send them to the customer at no additional cost. His leap-of-faith assumption proved correct, and investing in the business was all of a sudden less risky. This company became Zappos, which was acquired by Amazon for $850m in 2017.
