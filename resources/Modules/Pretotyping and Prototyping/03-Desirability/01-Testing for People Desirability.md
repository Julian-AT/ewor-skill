# Testing for People Desirability 

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/gPAGJ46Eij8)

For most solutions, the first leap-of-faith assumption is whether people actually want the solution. So the first pretotype that you built will likely test a hypothesis related to desirability.

> Most prototypes are built to answer questions like 'Can we build it?' or 'Will it work as expected?' rather than focusing on questions like 'Should we build it at all?' or 'If we build it, will people buy it and use it?'


## Proof of Value

People desirability assumptions generally fall into one of two categories: initial levels of interest, and sustained levels of interest. To understand if your solution is desirable, you will have to seek answers to questions like:

- Does the solution solve a relevant problem?
- Will the solution create added and unique value?
- Will the solution attract return traffic?
- Does the solution fit the mental model of the target user or customer?
- Does my solution significantly improve the lives of users and customers, compelling them to switch from their current solution to ours?

Though, the exact questions highly depend on your solution and your leap-of-faith assumption.

## The Story of Unlimitix

Daniel founded Unlimitix, a company that uses AI chatbots to guide people on their weight loss journey. After ideating the solution, Daniel and his team identified their leap-of-faith assumption: users would come back to the chat if they had received good answers to their nutritional questions.

They designed a simple prototype: The team would converse with their target audience over Facebook Messenger. Note that both Facebook Messenger, as well as conversations with team members, were not part of the solution they envisioned. However, this method allowed them to test the hypothesis fast and cheaply.

It turned out that people did not return to the chat. Daniel and his team iterated on their idea and found out that people did return to the chat if they first engaged in a conversation understanding the problem of the person they were chatting with. They had learned how to adjust their solution in such a way that they could count on sustained levels of interest.
