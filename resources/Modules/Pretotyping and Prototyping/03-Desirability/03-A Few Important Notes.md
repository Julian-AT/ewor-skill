Before we move on to how to build a fake door pretotype yourself, we wanted to share a few important notes.

## A Note on Ethics

Many of the pretotypes described above have people believe that there is a real solution being offered.

**While it is important for people to believe the solution is real to gather the highest quality data, you can quickly run into ethical problems.**

### A Look at the Second-Day Sushi Example

Let’s have a look at the second-day sushi, for example.

If someone did try to buy the sushi with the 50% off sticker, they could either be told the sticker is not valid and would still have to pay full price, or the cashier would indeed give the person 50% off, resulting in a loss in revenue and profit for the café. There are several ways you can deal with this.

First, you either stop the person trying to buy the sushi and tell them it was an experiment and this is actually fresh sushi. You could offer to pay for the sushi and ask them if they would be willing to answer some more questions. Yet another solution is to discuss your experiment with the café and offer to pay the missed revenue of each of the 50% off sushi they sold.

In general, we recommend never leaving people (feeling) cheated. Do they click the ‘buy’ button on your fake door website? Make sure they see a nice pop-up saying the product is still being developed and that you would love to sign them up for the waitlist and offer a discount on the product. At such an early stage of testing, it is important to cherish those who are trying to buy your product.

## A Note on Fancy Pretotype Names

Don’t be overwhelmed by the fancy names of these pretotypes. It is just jargon, so people understand each other better. Think of them like ‘ultrasound’, ‘PETscan’, or ‘MRI’. 

You don’t go to the hospital with the goal of getting an ultrasound. They perform an ultrasound because it is the most economical tool they have to get the information they need about your condition.

Do any of the above-mentioned examples fit perfectly with what you are trying to prove? Great! By all means, copy any of those pretotype versions. It is however way more likely that you have to design your own test and that your pretotype will only vaguely resemble what is discussed here (and in the next lessons).

## A Note on What to Measure

The proof of desirability is when users and customers are changing their behaviours. Hopefully, in the way you envisioned them to. This could be doing something different than they used to or doing something completely new. One of the best proof for that behaviour change is when your test subjects are opening their wallets. As you saw in the examples, many of them ask for actual money. If possible, try to do that as early as possible. Most other behaviours you can measure (like signing up for a waitlist) are a lot less commitment and although it is preferred over opinions, nothing beats a click on the ‘buy’ button (which, btw, also tests your pricing at the same time).

**General rule:** The best proof of desirability is users and customers opening their wallets.

## A Note on Business Viability

A test to prove desirability is hardly ever just that. Even if you don’t mean to, you are often testing elements of your business model such as the distribution channel, pricing model, brand identity etcetera. Sometimes, your distribution channel is part of the innovative idea, such as for CarsDirect (leap-of-faith assumption: people would buy second-hand cars online), but that is not always the case.

In the WalHub example, the product took advantage of the existing IKEA brand and people’s mental model of IKEA seemed to align with the product. However, if Musk had tried to collect cheques for entering the waitlist for his electric car in a Walmart car park, that likely wouldn’t have worked.

Be aware of which business model-related elements you are testing with your pretotype. If your test results are not what you expect them to be, run the test again but change some of these elements. Would the second-day sushi sell if it was even cheaper? Test it.
