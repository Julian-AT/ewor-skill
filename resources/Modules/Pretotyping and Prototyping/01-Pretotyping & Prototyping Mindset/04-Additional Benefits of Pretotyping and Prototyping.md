# Additional Benefits of Pretotyping and Prototyping

[YouTube](https://youtu.be/VBdsaceRp5M)

So far, we’ve focused on pretotyping and prototyping as tools for testing the desirability, viability, and feasibility of your solution. But that’s only part of the story. These methods don’t just help you avoid building the wrong thing—they also unlock hidden benefits that shape how you think, work, and communicate as a founder.

**1. Reducing the Cost of Failure**

Startups are experiments by nature—and most experiments fail. That’s not a problem if you fail early, learn fast, and keep moving. Pretotyping and prototyping help you do exactly that. They place you in a continuous feedback loop with real users, allowing you to quickly discard what doesn’t work and double down on what does. Sure, not every idea will land—but instead of wasting months (and thousands of euros or dollars) building something people don’t want, you’ll fail fast, cheap, and with valuable insights in hand.

> You cannot reduce failure chances. But you can reduce failure costs.

**2. Exploring the Tangible**

When you build something—even a low-fidelity version—it forces clarity. You go from abstract thinking to hands-on iteration. Small things get surfaced: unclear assumptions, design mismatches, usability gaps. You and your team are much more likely to align when you’re looking at something real. Even without market input, prototyping sharpens your thinking.

**3. The Psychological Effect**

It’s easy to get stuck in your head or lose steam when building something new. But there’s a powerful mental shift that happens when you prototype—even if it’s rough or incomplete. Research by Gerber and Carroll shows that low-fidelity prototyping helps people reframe failure as progress. Every test becomes a learning opportunity, not a personal setback. This mindset keeps you moving forward, even when things feel uncertain.

Prototyping also builds confidence. Each tangible version of your idea—no matter how scrappy—reinforces your belief that you can do this. That momentum is invaluable, especially in the early stages when energy and motivation matter most.

**4. Inspiring Others and Building Credibility**

Pretotypes and prototypes aren’t just testing tools—they also help you inspire others. Early hires are more likely to join if they can see what you're building. Investors, too, prefer working versions over slides or mockups. Even better, they trust data from real interactions far more than expert opinions, market trends, or projected financials. A simple test, combined with clear learnings and iteration, says more about your potential than any pitch deck

> For every one of our failures, we had spreadsheets that looked awesome.
