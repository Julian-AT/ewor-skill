As the Co-Founder & Managing Director of PROJECT EADEN, he leads a mission to make saving the planet a pleasure, not a compromise. With a background in founding and managing successful ventures like mymuesli, where he served as Co-Founder & Managing Director for over 15 years, Hubertus brings extensive experience in building sustainable businesses. He is also a member of the Advisory Board at SIRPLUS and the Supervisory Board at mymuesli. Hubertus is committed to creating a better world through innovative and impactful initiatives.

## Recommendations

In the video, Hubertus recommends some books and articles. You can find those below:


- **Reinventing Organizations by Frederic Laloux**
In this book, Laloux introduces a different set of management principles and practices related to organizing work and organizations. In [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcS04BI2sbk), Laloux explains the principles as described in the book.
- **Non-violent Communication by Marshall Rosenberg**
American psychologist Marshall Rosenberg details a communication process aimed at peaceful resolution of conflicts. The book is full of helpful tips and frameworks for providing feedback and having difficult conversations.
- **Team of Teams by Stanley McChrystal**
Written by retired US Army General Stanley McChrystal, the book discusses a decentralized model of organizing your teams to enhance communication and efficiency.
- **Why Employees Are Always a Bad Idea by Chuck Blakeman**
In this book, Chuck Blakeman makes a case for transforming employees into stakeholders. Blakeman details why managerial practices from the industrial age, such as the 9 to 5 workday and scheduled lunch breaks, won’t work for modern companies and young employees. Instead, he talks about new managerial practices that work well in the participation age: no departments, no titles, no managers, and no employees.
