# Ideation Techniques

In the previous lesson, we discussed creating ‘How Might We’ questions using your synthesis result. These HMW questions serve as the launchpad for ideation. In this topic, we’ll discuss two popular ideation techniques: Brainstorming and Brainwriting. Both are aimed at fostering unconventional connections between existing ideas and both leverage HMW questions to spark innovative responses.

## Brainstorming

Brainstorming, a staple of collaborative ideation, encourages participants to freely share ideas aloud in a dynamic group setting. Typically, a facilitator guides the session, ensuring a free flow of thoughts and preventing premature judgment. Ideas are often captured on a whiteboard or digital equivalent, allowing for visual organization and later refinement.

Advantages:

- Group interaction can spark unexpected connections and build momentum.
- Allows for immediate clarification and elaboration of ideas.
- Good for high-energy and fast-paced environments.

## Brainwriting

Developed by marketing expert Bernd Rohrbach in 1969, brainwriting offers a structured, silent alternative to traditional brainstorming. Participants individually record their ideas in writing, eliminating the potential for verbal dominance and fostering a more inclusive environment. After a designated period, participants exchange their written ideas, either by passing notes or posting them on a shared board. This exchange allows for the refinement and expansion of initial concepts.

Advantages:

- Accommodates introverted individuals and minimises groupthink.
- Silent generation can lead to a higher volume of ideas.
- Written expression can be less intimidating than verbal articulation.
