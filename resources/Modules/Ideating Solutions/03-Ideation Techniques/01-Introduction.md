# Introduction

In ideation, the goal is to get your mind to combine old ideas in unexpected and less obvious ways.

Your ideation process starts with the Point of View (POV) you developed in the 'A Problem Worth Solving' module. However, your POV might be quite broad, as it likely highlights a major challenge or opportunity. Our brains struggle to generate ideas for broad problems—big questions rarely have immediate, clear answers.  

To tackle this, we’ll break your synthesis down into a series of smaller, more specific questions. This makes ideation more manageable and helps spark better, and more ideas, both for you and those brainstorming with you.  

Breaking an opportunity into smaller, digestible parts makes ideation more approachable. These smaller questions act as stepping stones, allowing you to generate concrete answers to each. While tackling a big problem all at once can be overwhelming, we’re much better at piecing together smaller solutions to form a meaningful, larger one.
