# It’s a Numbers Game

The goal of the ideation phase is to generate as many ideas as possible—aiming for at least 100. At this stage, quality doesn’t matter; the focus is purely on quantity, so hold off on any judgment until the selection process. As Thomas Edison famously said,

> To come up with a good idea, I have lots of them.

There are several reasons why generating a large number of ideas is crucial:

### Quality Distribution

The quality of ideas often follows a normal distribution, with most being average, some poor, and a few brilliant. By generating more ideas, the likelihood of finding those brilliant ones increases.

![The ideas you generate follow a normal distribution](/api/media/file/M3-L2T2-InvertedUCurve)

### The Creative Cliff Illusion

Your best ideas rarely come early in the process. At first, the obvious ones will surface—copies of existing concepts or predictable combinations. But as your brain pushes further, it is forced to dig deeper, increasing the likelihood of discovering more unique and unexpected connections.

This idea may seem counterintuitive. Many believe that the best ideas come early, inspired by stories of sudden *“aha”* moments or flashes of genius in the shower. However, researchers Loran Nordgren and Brian Lucas of Kellogg University have identified a cognitive bias called the [creative cliff illusion](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2005620117).

In their study, they asked research subjects to predict their creativity for each minute of a 5-minute brainstorm and then actually made them brainstorm for the entire 5 minutes.  Most expected it to decline over time. However, when a separate group of participants later evaluated the ideas for creativity, they found that the ones generated later were actually more creative. This suggests that creativity doesn’t fade with time—it often improves the longer you push your thinking.

![The Creative Cliff Illusion (adopted from Loran Nordgren and Brain Lucas)](/api/media/file/M3-L1T2-CreativeCliffIllusion)

### Evaluation

The more ideas you generate, the easier it becomes to assess their quality. Having a larger pool of ideas allows for better comparisons, making it simpler to distinguish between the good and the bad.

### Confidence Boost

Generating a large number of ideas can boost your confidence, which in turn fuels even more creative thinking. With greater confidence, you become bolder and more willing to take risks, leading to more daring and experimental ideas.

### Prevent Emotional Attachment

It's important to avoid becoming emotionally attached to a single idea. When that happens, confirmation bias kicks in, making it seem like all signs point to success—even when they don’t. To stay as objective as possible, generate a wide range of ideas and don’t stop until you have fallen in love at least three times.
