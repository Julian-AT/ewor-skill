# Introduction

In the previous module, you were tasked with absorbing input, much like a sponge soaking up information, about an unmet need through interviews, observations, and immersions. Now, it's time to wring out that sponge and translate those insights into concrete solutions. This module will guide you through the process of generating ideas for solutions and creating concepts out of those ideas to solve the problem you identified in "A Problem Worth Solving".

This module is right for you if you:

- Need some help coming up with creative solutions for the opportunity.
- Have lots of potential solutions in mind, but don’t know how to evaluate them.

### What Makes a Promising Solution?

At the end of this module, you will have identified several solutions and will have picked the most promising one with which you will start the next module: Pretotyping & Prototyping. Here, we will help you determine whether the solution is in fact a solution to build using the following criteria:

- The solution solves the problem you identified.
- The solution is technically feasible.
- The solution can be developed, sold, and delivered in such a way that enough value can be captured for the company.
- The solution differs meaningfully from available solutions by competitors.
- You and your team collectively possess the required skills to develop and market the solution.
- You can see yourself working on this solution and the resulting business for at least 10 years.
- You are excited about the solution.
