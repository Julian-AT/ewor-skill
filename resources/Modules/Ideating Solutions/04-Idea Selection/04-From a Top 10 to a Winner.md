# From a Top 10 to a Winner

The final step in idea selection is to select the winning idea with which you will start prototyping. We will discuss three ways of further reducing your ideas to select the winner: reviewing the competitive landscape, mental simulation and reflection.

### Differentiation from competitors

Up until now, we haven’t discussed the uniqueness of the ideas in your top 10. Research your main competitors for each of your Top 10 ideas.  If you find competitors offering the same or a very similar solution, determine if you still have a competitive edge. Perhaps you have a geographic advantage, unique skills, or valuable connections. Rate your top 10 ideas based on their innovation and potential for differentiation.

### Mental simulation

For each of your Top 10 ideas, consider what an average day or average week would look like executing that idea. How many people would be in the team? What type of people would that be? What would be required to acquire customers? How much money would you have to raise? What would you be doing on a day-to-day basis? After answering these questions for each of your ideas, it is likely that some won’t seem as promising anymore.

### Reflection

You have scored all the ideas you had based on many different criteria. Go over your top 10 and reflect on your scores. Do you still feel the same? Do some ideas seem to score too high or too low based on everything you have learned? Is there an idea that excites you and that you keep coming back to?

## Pick the Winner

Based on your review of the competitive landscape, the mental simulation and your reflection, pick the idea that speaks to you the most. With this idea, you will start prototyping. If you can’t decide between a few great ideas, pick the one that is easiest to develop prototypes for. 

Make sure to store your spreadsheet with all your ideas, so you can always return to it and select another idea if your initial pick turns out to be less promising than expected.
