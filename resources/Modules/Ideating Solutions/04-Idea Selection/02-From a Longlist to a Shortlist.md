# From a Longlist to a Shortlist

The first step in idea selection is to narrow down your long list of ideas to a shorter and more manageable list.

Our preferred way to do that is to use the criteria from the innovation Venn diagram we have seen before. As you know, a promising startup offers a product that people want and is technologically feasible. In addition, the business model should be viable. These three criteria can help you make a quick and dirty first selection among all the ideas you have gathered.

## People Desirability

Evaluate each idea against the point of view you defined earlier. Does it truly solve the problem?

To track this, add a column to your idea spreadsheet and score each idea on how effectively it addresses the problem. A simple scoring system is sufficient, as the goal is to identify the top 5-20 ideas, not to rank them meticulously. Keep all ideas for potential future use.

## Technical Feasibility

Next, assess the technical feasibility of each idea. For many ideas, this will be readily apparent. Note your assessment in a separate column in your spreadsheet. If you're unsure, do some quick research online or consult industry experts (including EWOR Mentors). The goal is a quick scan of your ideas, not a deep dive into technical details. Spend no more than a few minutes per idea on this step.

## Business Viability

Finally, evaluate the business viability of each idea. This can be challenging, as many solutions could have multiple business models.

Focus on eliminating ideas that clearly won't lead to a viable business. We also recommend eliminating all ideas that require business models with limited scalability as Venture Capitalists look for startups that can hyperscale.

EWOR Mentors can provide valuable insights into business viability, given their experience with startups and market dynamics. They can offer guidance on factors like customer acquisition costs, service costs, and customer lifetime value.

![In separate columns, mark whether the idea solves the problem, is technically feasible and can result in a viable business](/api/media/file/M3-L4T2-TableForRatingIdeas)

After you have scored all your ideas on these three metrics, you will likely end up with a list that is between 50 and 80% shorter than your long list. At this stage, it is important to eliminate ideas that do not solve the customer problem, are not technically feasible, or have a low likelihood of having a viable business model.

Next, we will discuss how to reduce this list even further.

## Useful Tools

**Business Model Navigator**
[Business Model Navigator](https://businessmodelnavigator.com/) is a helpful website that categorized and named 55 business models. The business model is described and examples of well-known companies are added. Take a look to see if there is a business model or multiple business models that can work well with your ideas.
